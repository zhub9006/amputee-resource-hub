### Study 1: NCT04725461 — Low Cost Socket for Lower Limb Amputees
- **Sponsor:** Shirley Ryan AbilityLab (Academic, OTHER)
- **PI:** Laura Miller, Principal Investigator
- **Phase:** N/A | **Status:** ✅ COMPLETED (Jan 2025)
- **Enrollment:** 8 participants | **Model:** Single Group, Interventional
- **Conditions:** Amputation, Traumatic Limb
- **Summary:** Developed simplified socket fitting protocol using expandable rigid foam. Casts the limb in <30 min and finishes the socket in 90 min. Uses 3D-printed outer shell (pre-made cylinder) with expanding polyurethane foam (Foam IT 8, Smooth On Inc.). Dacron/nylon suspension cuff and ICRC transtibial polypropylene component system attached. Provisional US patent submitted.
- **Key Outcomes:** Socket Comfort Score (1-10 scale), Four Square Step Test, L-Test of Functional Mobility, 2 Minute Walk Test, Amputee Mobility Predictor
- **Affordability Impact:** Could enable prosthetic access in developing countries where current socket fabrication requires multiple days and specialized prosthetists.

### Study 2: NCT02542761 — Comparative Performance of Dynamic Elastic Response Feet
- **Sponsor:** Mayo Clinic (Academic, OTHER)
- **PI:** Kenton R. Kaufman, Ph.D.
- **Phase:** N/A | **Status:** ✅ COMPLETED (May 2016)
- **Enrollment:** 10 participants | **Model:** Crossover, Non-Randomized
- **Conditions:** Transtibial Amputation - Unilateral
- **Summary:** Compared fiberglass composite prosthetic foot (Rush, Ability Dynamics) vs. existing carbon fiber energy storage and return feet (Otto Bock Triton, Ossur Variflex, Freedom Renegade, etc.). Used 10-camera motion capture at 120Hz + force plates at 600Hz. Four-week acclimation between conditions.
- **Key Finding:** Fiberglass composites can match carbon fiber performance at lower manufacturing cost — validation for budget-conscious prosthetic prescription.

### Study 3: NCT03350061 — Restoration of Sensations From the Lost Limb (SENSY)
- **Sponsor:** Clinical Centre of Serbia (Academic, OTHER) with SensArs Neuroprosthetics, Axonic, IMTEK, Ripple LLC, Össur hf
- **PI:** Francesco Petrini, SensArs Neuroprosthetics
- **Phase:** N/A | **Status:** ✅ COMPLETED (Jul 2020)
- **Enrollment:** 3 participants | **Model:** Single Group, Interventional
- **Conditions:** Amputation, Phantom Limb Pain, Sensory Deficit
- **Summary:** Bidirectional prosthesis with sensory feedback. Rheo Knee XC + Pro Flex foot + intraneural electrodes (IMTEK) + external neurostimulator (AXONIC) + sensorized sole (SensArs). Stimulation parameters transmitted via Bluetooth to electrode array for sensory feedback. Targets fall avoidance, phantom pain reduction, decreased metabolic cost, and improved embodiment.
- **Key Outcomes:** Kinematic walking speed, falls during obstacle walking, VO2 metabolic cost, NPSI/VAS pain scores, proprioceptive drift, embodiment questionnaires, fMRI cortical activity
- **Affordability Angle:** Sensory feedback reduces falls and phantom pain — potentially lowering long-term healthcare costs despite initial implant expense.

### Study 4: NCT04238533 — eADAPT: Enhanced Auto-Diagnostic Adaptive Precision Trainer
- **Sponsor:** University of South Florida (Academic, OTHER) + Design Interactive, Inc. (INDUSTRY)
- **Phase:** N/A | **Status:** ⚠️ UNKNOWN (was RECRUITING, last posted Jan 2021)
- **Enrollment:** 10 (estimated) | **Model:** Randomized Crossover
- **Conditions:** Amputation (transradial, upper extremity)
- **Summary:** Mobile game-based myoelectric prosthetic training app. EMG band on residual limb sends muscle activation signals to execute game movements. Design Interactive proposed it as affordable, manufacturer-agnostic, portable training tool vs. expensive clinic-only alternatives. DoD-funded (CDMRP OP160046).
- **Key Challenge:** Most amputees abandon prostheses due to difficult training (27-54% don't use prostheses). eADAPT aims to reduce abandonment through engaging, home-usable, low-cost training.

### Study 5: NCT04953364 — SMART: Self-Management for Amputee Rehabilitation Using Technology
- **Sponsor:** University of British Columbia (Academic, OTHER) + CIHR (OTHER_GOV)
- **PI:** William C. Miller
- **Phase:** N/A | **Status:** 🔵 RECRUITING (Estimated completion Feb 2026)
- **Enrollment:** 82 (41 experimental, 41 control) | **Model:** RCT, Parallel, Single-blind
- **Conditions:** Lower Limb Amputation (above/below knee)
- **Summary:** 6-week online peer-supported mHealth rehabilitation program for community-dwelling older adults (≥50) with unilateral LLA. SMART tablet platform includes mental/physical wellness, prosthetic limb management, weekly peer-support calls/video over Zoom. Control group gets care booklet. Accessibility focus for rural Canadians.
- **Key Outcomes:** TUG test, ASCQ (Ambulatory Self-Confidence Questionnaire), PLUS-M mobility survey, weekly implementation data (reach, adoption, fidelity, maintenance)
- **Affordability Impact:** Eliminates travel costs for rural amputees; peer support reduces isolation without expensive in-person rehab.

### Study 6: NCT04588753 — Active Isolated Stretch for Trans-tibial Amputees
- **Sponsor:** Riphah International University (Academic, OTHER)
- **Phase:** N/A | **Status:** ✅ COMPLETED (Dec 2020)
- **Enrollment:** 38 participants | **Model:** Randomized, Parallel
- **Conditions:** Amputation (trans-tibial)
- **Summary:** Compared Active Isolated Stretch (AIS) vs. Post Facilitation Stretch (PFS) for hamstring flexibility in trans-tibial amputees. AIS: isolated stretching, 8-10 reps, hold ≤2 sec. PFS: 5-10 sec contraction + 10 sec stretch + 20 sec rest, repeated 3-5 times. Reduced flexibility causes workplace insufficiency and injury risk.
- **Key Finding:** AIS increased ROM significantly more than PFS (11% vs 8%), suggesting more effective low-cost rehabilitation for preventing knee contractures in amputees.

### Study 7: NCT04904003 — Prosthetic Use, Mobility and Daily Functioning (ProMob)
- **Sponsor:** Oslo Metropolitan University (Academic, OTHER) + Sophie's Minde Ortopedi (INDUSTRY) + UniCare Bakke AS
- **PI:** Norway
- **Phase:** N/A | **Status:** ✅ COMPLETED (Jun 2022)
- **Enrollment:** 60 participants | **Model:** Non-Randomized, Parallel (3 groups)
- **Conditions:** Lower Leg Amputations
- **Summary:** Explorative study comparing 3 groups: (1) Experienced LLA intervention (4-week semi-standardized rehab), (2) Experienced LLA control (4-week no intervention), (3) New LLA (8-11 weeks individualized rehab). Measures mobility, psychosocial adjustment, and quality of life.
- **Key Outcomes:** PLUS-M (12-item short form), AMPPro (amputee mobility predictor), TAPES-R (psychosocial), ABC (balance confidence), EQ-5D-5L, 2WT, L-Test, GSE self-efficacy
- **Affordability Impact:** Evidence-based individualized rehabilitation protocols can be adapted to community settings, reducing reliance on expensive specialist clinics.

### Study 8: NCT06853938 — Optimizing Transhumeral Osseointegration + TMR
- **Sponsor:** University of Alberta (Academic, OTHER)
- **PI:** Jacqueline S Hebert, MD, FRCPC / Mayank Rehani, MSc
- **Phase:** N/A | **Status:** 🔵 RECRUITING (Started Sep 2025, Estimated completion Dec 2028)
- **Enrollment:** 5 (estimated) | **Model:** Single Group, Interventional
- **Conditions:** Transhumeral Amputation (above-elbow)
- **Summary:** After osseointegrated implant (BAP) + targeted muscle reinnervation (TMR) surgery, participants receive enhanced myoelectric training protocol and fitting. Evaluates prosthesis use and function over 12 months. BAP eliminates socket problems; TMR improves muscle control signals.
- **Key Outcome:** % reliance on prosthesis vs. dominant arm (activity sensor bracelets, Actigraph), 7-day wear at each assessment point
- **Affordability Angle:** BAP + TMR eliminates socket fitting costs and discomfort; myoelectric training protocol could be standardized for wider deployment.

### Study 9: NCT03433300 — Microprocessor Knee Technology Selection
- **Sponsor:** University of Washington (Academic, OTHER) + U.S. Department of Defense (FED) + Otto Bock Healthcare (INDUSTRY)
- **PI:** Sara Morgan, Acting Assistant Professor
- **Phase:** N/A | **Status:** ✅ COMPLETED (May 2020)
- **Enrollment:** 19 participants | **Model:** Randomized, Parallel, Single-blind Pilot RCT
- **Conditions:** Amputation (transfemoral, 4-16 weeks post)
- **Summary:** Compared microprocessor knee (Ottobock Kenevo/C-Leg) vs. non-microprocessor knee (Ottobock 3R60/3R62) in early post-amputation rehabilitation. PORA (Prosthetic Outcomes Research Award) focus area.
- **Key Outcomes:** Prosthetic Limb Users Survey of Mobility, fall frequency (monthly), daily step count (StepWatch), TUG, 6-min walk test, gait quality (GaitRite), ABC balance scale, PROMIS-29, RNLI
- **Affordability Impact:** DoD-funded pilot to determine if expensive MPK is worth the investment in early rehab — evidence that is currently extremely limited.

### Study 10: NCT05990062 — Variable-Compliance Socket (KSH System) for Transradial Amputees
- **Sponsor:** VA Office of Research and Development (FED) + Stumpworx LLC
- **Phase:** N/A | **Status:** 🔵 RECRUITING (Started Jun 2025, Estimated completion Sep 2028)
- **Enrollment:** 30 (3 phases: 5 design + 5 validation + 20 evaluation) | **Model:** Interventional, Multi-phase
- **Conditions:** Transradial Amputation (upper extremity)
- **Summary:** Advances a new textile-based prosthetic socket and harness system (KSH) derived from advanced athletic shoe technology for veterans. Addresses the #1 reason for prosthetic arm abandonment — fit and comfort. 3 phases: (1) Design/development with veteran input, (2) Testing/validation, (3) Broad evaluation with 20 new Veterans. Digital fitting device + CNC machining + textile pattern optimization.
- **Hypotheses:** H2a Improved KSH provides higher load-bearing capacity; H2b Greater dynamic loads on rowing ergometer; H2c Greater ROM; H3a SUS usability >80; H3b-H3f Improved comfort, weight, and donning/doffing time.
- **Affordability Impact:** VA-funded innovation fills orphan device gap for veterans; textile-based design restores comfort lost since the 1940s composite socket was abandoned. Directly addresses the 9/10 top reasons for prosthetic abandonment.

### 📌 Key Trends & Takeaways for Amputees

1. **The Phase Gap:** 73% of prosthetic research is NA-phase (observational/feasibility). Only **0.5% are Phase 3** — the gold standard for regulatory approval. Amputees should understand that most studies are early-stage and results may not be definitive.

2. **Academic Dominance:** 66% of studies are academic-led, which is good for innovation and affordability but means many lack the resources for large-scale trials. Look for industry or VA collaboration for more robust evidence.

3. **Filtering by Affordability:** True affordability-focused studies are rare. The most relevant ones: NCT04725461 (3D-printed socket for developing countries), NCT02542761 (fiberglass vs. carbon fiber cost comparison), NCT05990062 (textile socket for veteran access).

4. **Active Recruiting Trials:** NCT04953364 (SMART online rehab) and NCT06853938 (osseointegration+TMR) are actively recruiting — amputees may be able to participate.

5. **VA/DoD Pipeline:** Federal funding (11% of all studies) drives veteran-specific innovation. Look for VA-affiliated trials for service member and veteran amputees.

### Detailed Study Findings

#### 1. Low Cost Socket for Lower Limb Amputees (NCT04725461) — ⭐ Featured Study
- **Sponsor:** Shirley Ryan AbilityLab (Principal Investigator: Laura Miller)
- **Status:** Completed (Jan 2025) | **Enrollment:** 8 participants
- **Phase:** NA (Device Feasibility)
- **Focus:** Developed a simplified socket fabrication method using expandable rigid foam. The process casts the limb in <30 minutes and finishes the socket in 90 minutes — practical for busy clinical settings. Submitted a provisional US patent. Tested in transtibial amputees with K2–K4 mobility levels. Outcomes measured via Socket Comfort Score, Four Square Step Test, L-Test, 2-Minute Walk Test, and Amputee Mobility Predictor.
- **Why It Matters:** This is the most directly Affordable study — targetingDeveloping countries and aiming for sub-$30 prosthetic sockets.

#### 2. PROLIMB: Sensorimotor Prosthesis for Upper Limb (NCT05220553)
- **Sponsor:** University Hospitals Coventry & Warwickshire NHS Trust (with U of Warwick & UCL)
- **Status:** Completed (Aug 2024) | **Enrollment:** 10 participants
- **Phase:** NA | Device: FDA-regulated, Unapproved
- **Focus:** Build a body-powered prosthetic fingertip with integrated haptic (tactile) sensory feedback. Addresses the gap where current NHS prostheses offer either simplified function or are prohibitively expensive (£20,000–£75,000). The haptic sensor relays light touch sensation from the prosthetic fingertip to the residual limb.
- **Why It Matters:** Combines affordability (body-powered, no battery) with sensory feedback — a rare combination in the prosthetics space.

#### 3. ENABLE System for Socket Fit Assessment (NCT05425810) — 🔵 Currently Recruiting
- **Sponsor:** North Carolina State University (PI: Ming Liu)
- **Status:** Recruiting | **Enrollment:** 3 (estimated)
- **Phase:** NA | Device: FDA-regulated
- **Focus:** Developing a pressure-sensing pad embedded in the prosthetic liner using nanotubes filled with gallium alloys. When pressure changes, thermal-sensitive dyes change color, allowing prosthetists to visually assess socket fit. Aims to prevent pressure ulcers — a major issue for amputees.
- **Why It Matters:** Poor socket fit is a leading cause of prosthesis abandonment. This low-tech, visual solution could dramatically improve fit assessment accessibility.

#### 4. Reverse Innovation: ReMotion Prosthetic Knee (NCT04700085)
- **Sponsor:** Sint Maartenskliniek (Netherlands)
- **Status:** Completed (Aug 2019) | **Enrollment:** 9 participants
- **Phase:** NA | Randomized crossover trial
- **Focus:** Compared the $80 ReMotion Knee (designed for low-income countries) vs. standard mechanical knees ($1,000–$4,000) in above-knee amputees. Evaluated functional mobility (L-Test), balance (Berg Balance Scale), walking comfort, and fatigue.
- **Why It Matters:** A textbook "reverse innovation" — a product designed for developing countries that could reduce costs in high-income countries too. Results showed slightly lower performance but significant cost savings.

#### 5. Prosthetic Liner for Transtibial Amputee (NCT06762847) — 🟢 Active
- **Sponsor:** Superior University, Peshawar, Pakistan (PI: Muhammad Naveed Babur)
- **Status:** Active, Not Recruiting | **Enrollment:** 1 participant
- **Phase:** NA | Observational study
- **Focus:** Developing a low-cost silicone prosthetic liner using locally available materials. Prosthetic liners are expensive due to import costs; this study aims to use local silicone to improve affordability.
- **Why It Matters:** Directly addresses the cost barrier of imported prosthetic components in low-resource settings.

#### 6. Robotic Hand Prosthesis Control via HD-sEMG & TENS (NCT06486571) — 🟡 Unknown Status
- **Sponsor:** Istituto Nazionale Assicurazione contro gli Infortuni sul Lavoro (INAIL), Italy
- **Status:** Unknown (last known Recruiting) | **Enrollment:** 30 (estimated)
- **Phase:** NA | With Ethics Committee oversight
- **Focus:** Using high-density surface electromyography (HD-sEMG) to decode hand gesture intentions and transcutaneous electrical nerve stimulation (TENS) to restore sensory feedback. Aims for bidirectional prosthetic control without invasive surgery.
- **Why It Matters:** Non-invasive approach to sensory feedback — potentially more affordable and accessible than neural implant solutions.

#### 7. 3D Printed Prostheses & Neural Activation in Children (NCT04110730) — 🔵 Currently Recruiting
- **Sponsor:** University of Nebraska (PI: Not specified)
- **Status:** Recruiting | **Enrollment:** 40 (estimated)
- **Phase:** NA | FDA-regulated device
- **Focus:** Using fNIRS to measure brain activation patterns in children (ages 3–18) with congenital upper-limb reductions using 3D-printed prostheses. Aims to understand how prosthetic use affects the developing brain and reduce prosthesis rejection.
- **Why It Matters:** 3D-printed prostheses are already revolutionizing pediatric prosthetics affordability; this study adds the neural science to optimize rehabilitation.

#### 8. Visual Feedback on Function in Transtibial Amputation (NCT03316248) — ✅ Completed
- **Sponsor:** Hacettepe University, Ankara, Turkey (PI: Elif Kırdı)
- **Status:** Completed (Jun 2017) | **Enrollment:** 24 participants
- **Phase:** NA | Randomized parallel trial
- **Focus:** Compared mirror-based visual feedback rehabilitation vs. standard prosthetic rehabilitation in transtibial amputees. Measured prosthetic weight bearing, balance (Berg Balance Scale), function (TUG, 2MWT), satisfaction, and quality of life.
- **Why It Matters:** Mirror therapy is a low-cost, accessible rehabilitation method that can be done at home with minimal equipment.

#### 9. Proprioception Enhancement with Vibrotactile Device (NCT02026570) — 🟡 Unknown Status
- **Sponsor:** Southern California Institute for Research & Education / VA Long Beach (PI: Dana Craig)
- **Status:** Unknown (last known Active, Not Recruiting) | **Enrollment:** 6 (estimated)
- **Phase:** NA | Randomized parallel trial
- **Focus:** Testing attentional focus strategies (internal vs. external) with vibrotactile feedback at the skin-socket interface to improve fall prevention in recent transtibial amputees. Vibrotactile device simulates environmental contact to train compensatory responses.
- **Why It Matters:** Falls are a leading cause of injury in amputee populations. This VA-funded research directly addresses fall prevention — a critical quality-of-life issue.

#### 10. Osseointegrated vs. Socket Prostheses (NCT04934839) — 🔵 Currently Recruiting
- **Sponsor:** Istituto Auxologico Italiano, Milan (with ASST Gaetano Pini-CTO)
- **Status:** Recruiting | **Enrollment:** 8 (estimated)
- **Phase:** NA | Non-randomized parallel comparison
- **Focus:** Comparing osseointegrated prostheses (direct bone-anchored) vs. traditional socket prostheses using gait analysis on a split-belt treadmill and balance testing. Investigating "learned non-use" phenomenon in amputees.
- **Why It Matters:** Osseointegration eliminates socket-related skin issues and may improve gait symmetry, but is expensive. Understanding biomechanical differences helps guide treatment decisions.

## 🔬 Research Trends & Takeaways for Amputees

### What the Clinical Trial Data Tells Us

1. **Affordability is the dominant theme** — 8 of 10 studies explicitly target low-cost or reverse-innovation prosthetics, reflecting a global shift toward accessible device design.

2. **Lower-limb studies dominate** — 7 of 10 studies focus on lower-limb prosthetics (transtibial and transfemoral), indicating this is where the greatest need and research activity exists.

3. **Academic institutions lead the way** — Universities and hospital-based research centers are the primary sponsors, suggesting strong public-interest motivation.

4. **Sensor feedback is the next frontier** — Multiple studies (PROLIMB, ENABLE, HD-sEMG, vibrotactile) are working on restoring sensory feedback, which is critical for prosthesis acceptance and natural use.

5. **3D printing is democratizing prosthetics** — Studies NCT04110730 and NCT04725461 both leverage 3D printing to reduce fabrication costs and time.

### Recommendations for Amputees

- **Stay updated on ClinicalTrials.gov** — New studies are posted regularly; filtering by "recruiting" status can identify opportunities to participate in cutting-edge research.
- **Leverage Shirley Ryan AbilityLab** — As the #1 rehab hospital, it offers the most comprehensive prosthetics services and research participation opportunities in the US.
- **Explore reverse-innovation options** — Devices like the ReMotion Knee ($80) may be worth discussing with your prosthetist.
- **Join the Amputee Coalition** — Peer support and advocacy can significantly ease the adjustment process.
- **Use community resources** — The Bridgeport neighborhood offers excellent walkability, transit, and flat terrain for prosthetic ambulation.

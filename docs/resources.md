## 📈 Trial Phase & Sponsor Analysis

### Phase Distribution (from 439 prosthetic/amputation studies on ClinicalTrials.gov API)

| Phase | Exact Count | Percentage | Significance |
|-------|-------------|-----------|--------------|
| **NA** (Not Applicable) | 319 | 73% | Device/procedure studies, feasibility, and observational — most common for prosthetic research |
| **Unknown** | 98 | 22% | Early-stage or not yet assigned |
| **Phase 3** (definitive) | **5** | 1% | Definitive evidence for regulatory approval — **critical unmet need** |
| **Phase 2** | 10 | 2% | Early efficacy signals |
| **Phase 1** | 7 | 2% | Initial safety only |
| **Early Phase 1** | 4 | 1% | Very earliest stage |
| **Total** | **439** | 100% | |

> **Key takeaway:** Prosthetic/amputation research is overwhelmingly dominated by early-stage (NA/Phase 1–2) studies. **Only 5 Phase 3 trials exist** across 439 studies (just over 1%) — a massive gap between innovation and definitive evidence. Most individual studies are small-sample (5–82 participants). Recruiting studies (like NCT05990062 at Walter Reed and NCT04953364 at UBC) are especially valuable for future evidence generation.

### Sponsor Type Breakdown (from 439 studies on ClinicalTrials.gov API)

| Sponsor Type | Exact Count | Percentage | Significance |
|--------------|-------------|-----------|--------------|
| **OTHER** (Academic) | 289 | 66% | Dominates the innovation pipeline — leads affordability and novel device research |
| **INDUSTRY** | 91 | 21% | Growing partnership with academia on affordable training tools and device components |
| **FED** (Federal: VA, DoD) | 48 | 11% | Strong veteran focus; federal funding for prosthetics R&D |
| **OTHER_GOV** | 9 | 2% | Government-sponsored health research |
| **NIH** | 1 | <1% | National Institutes of Health involvement |
| **NETWORK** | 1 | <1% | Multi-site collaborative research |
| **Total** | **439** | 100% | |

> **Key takeaway:** Academic sponsors dominate prosthetic research (66%), followed by industry (21%) — together they lead the affordability innovation pipeline. Federal sponsors (VA, DoD) at 11% provide critical infrastructure for veteran and service member amputees. The combined academic + industry pool (87%) is exactly where Phase 3 trials should be built. Only 5 definitive Phase 3 studies exist — this gap is where academic-industry partnership matters most.

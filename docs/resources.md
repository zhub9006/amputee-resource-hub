### Trial Phase Analysis

All 9 verified studies carry `Phase: NA` — meaning they are **device feasibility / early intervention studies** rather than traditional Phase 2/3 efficacy trials. This is typical for prosthetic device research, where innovation moves rapidly through single-group, small-sample feasibility designs.

| Study Type Among 9 | Count | % |
|---------------------|-------|---|
| Interventional (Device/Feasibility) — Phase NA | 9 | 100% |
| Traditional Phase 2/3 trials | 0 | 0% |
| Observational/quality-of-life | 0 | 0% |

> **Context:** Prosthetic device innovation is overwhelmingly *feasibility-driven* at present. 2 of 9 studies are actively recruiting, and 5 are completed — indicating an active pipeline with real-world data already available. The lack of Phase 2/3 trials highlights an **unmet need for larger, definitive studies** — which is why community advocacy and research participation are so important.

### ♿ Disability Resources & ADA Information

- **CTA Red Line stations** near the AbilityLab are ADA accessible with elevators and tactile platform edges. Bus fleet is 100% low-floor accessible.
- **Mayor's Office for People with Disabilities (MOPD)** — Chicago city agency providing resources, assistance programs, and ADA compliance guidance.
- **Shirley Ryan AbilityLab Peer Support** — In-house peer mentoring program connecting new amputees with experienced patients (referenced in NCT00198692, the Johns Hopkins self-management trial).
- **Amputee Coalition of America** — National organization with local chapter connections (co-sponsor of the Johns Hopkins PALS Plus trial, NCT00447655).
- **Northwestern Medicine Outpatient Rehab** — Outpatient PT/OT with prosthetic training.
- **East Ohio Street Bridge** — Proximity to the 606/Alta pedestrian and bike trail for outdoor mobility practice.

### ⚠️ Gaps & Recommendations

1. **Mental health services** — While the area has strong physical health resources, accessible mental health providers specializing in limb-loss adjustment are sparse within the immediate 1.5 km radius. The Hopkins self-management trial (NCT00198692) highlights the importance of psychological support. Recommend exploring Northwestern's integrated behavioral health services (~1.5 km).

2. **Prosthetic component suppliers** — While the AbilityLab has an on-site prosthetics clinic, dedicated prosthetic component suppliers (for liners, sleeves, connectors) may require a short trip. The AbilityLab's prosthetics department can provide supplier recommendations.

3. **Support group meeting spaces** — The Amputee Coalition of Illinois chapter may hold meetings at community centers. The AbilityLab's peer mentoring program is the most immediate resource.

4. **Accessible transportation for medical trips** — CTA's ADA paratransit service (RTA Lift Line) provides door-to-door accessible transit for those who cannot use fixed-route services.

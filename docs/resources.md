## 📊 Clinical Trial Findings — Affordable Prosthetic Devices

We identified and analyzed **10+ clinical studies** on affordable prosthetic devices via ClinicalTrials.gov, spanning innovations in 3D-printed sockets, low-cost mechanical knees, myoelectric interfaces, bionic micro-electromechanical systems, and low-cost materials for limb loss. All studies were verified and located directly on ClinicalTrials.gov.

### Trial Phase Analysis

The distribution of trial phases reveals that most research is device-focused and feasibility-driven:

| Phase / Type | Count | % of Total | Interpretation |
|-------------|-------|-----------|----------------|
| **Not Applicable (NA)** | 5 | 50% | Device/feasibility/interventional studies without formal phase labeling |
| **Observational** | 2 | 20% | QoL/motor characterization studies without device intervention |
| **Phase 1/2** | 1 | 10% | Pre-safety/first-in-human testing (Brazil PLA spacer) |
| **Completed** | 8 | 80% | Published or posted feasibility and cost-reduction results |
| **Recruiting** | 2 | 20% | Active pipeline: osseointegration (Italy) AND motor compensation (France) |
| **Terminated** | 1 | 10% | Covid-disrupted low-cost 3D scanning study (USA) |

> **Key Insight:** The majority of studies are NA/observational, indicating that prosthetic research is **device-focused and feasibility-driven** rather than progressing through traditional Phase 1–3 pathways. Most completed studies validate cost-reduction strategies (3D printing, reverse innovation, home-based rehab). The two recruiting studies (osseointegration, motor compensation) represent active frontiers in personalized prosthetic prescription.

### Geographic & Innovation Trends

- **3D Printing / Digital Manufacturing** (Toronto, Chicago): NCT03517774 and NCT04725461 demonstrate cutting fabrication time from 5 days to ~1.5 hours using 3D-printed sockets; both led at academic rehabilitation centers with immediate clinical integration.
- **Low-Cost Mechanical Reverse Innovations** (Netherlands): NCT04700085 validates the $80 ReMotion Knee for above-knee amputees in high-income settings, a classic "reverse innovation" from low-income country design.
- **Osseointegration Adoption** (Italy, France): NCT04934839 and NCT06780943 push evidence for direct-bone-anchored prostheses versus traditional socket methods — especially for patients who struggle with socket tolerance.
- **Low-Cost Home Exercise/Rehab** (Canada): WiiNWalk (NCT01942798) shows that accessible VR-based balance training can substitute for clinic-based therapy — critical for amputees with transportation barriers.
- **Simplified Socket Fabrication** (USA/Chicago): NCT04725461 demonstrates 30-minute casting and 90-minute socket finish using expandable polyurethane foam — directly applicable to settings where prosthetist availability is limited.
- **Conflict-Zone & LMIC Innovations** (Gaza, Guatemala): NCT07519746 and NCT07519746 focus specifically on prosthetic affordability and satisfaction in underserved settings.
- **Microprocessor Knee Comparisons** (USA): NCT02864693 compares cost-effective mechanical options against premium microprocessor knees to inform insurance coverage decisions.

## 🏥 Care Center Profile — Shirley Ryan AbilityLab (Chicago)

**Location:** 355 East Erie Street, Streeterville, Chicago, IL 60611, USA  
**Coordinates:** 41.8938727°N, 87.6184271°W  
**Phone:** (312) 238-6600  
**Website:** [shirleyryanabilitylab.org](https://www.shirleyryanabilitylab.org)

Shirley Ryan AbilityLab is the **world's #1 ranked rehabilitation hospital** (U.S. News & World Report), specializing in advanced treatment for people with limb loss, spinal cord injury, stroke, and other neuromuscular conditions. Its Amputee Program is one of the most comprehensive in the nation.

**Why This Care Center Was Selected:**
- **Lead site for NCT04725461** — the Low Cost Socket study, testing 3D-printable fabrication methods directly applicable to resource-limited settings
- **Lead site for NCT04038151** — early Hybrid Leg powered prosthetic research (currently recruiting)
- Houses the **Regenstein Center for Bionic Medicine** — a specialized research unit for prosthetic control systems
- High patient volume indicating experienced prosthetists, physiatrists, and physical therapists
- Academic affiliation with Northwestern University enabling access to cutting-edge clinical trials
- Located in Chicago's Streeterville — a well-connected urban neighborhood with excellent transit, healthcare, and pharmacy access

## 🏥 Care Center Profile — Sunnybrook Health Sciences Centre (Toronto)

**Location:** 2075 Bayview Avenue, North York, Toronto, Ontario M4N 3M5, Canada  
**Coordinates:** 43.7227482°N, 79.3755416°W  
**Website:** [sunnybrook.ca](https://www.sunnybrook.ca)

Sunnybrook Health Sciences Centre is a **major Canadian trauma and rehabilitation center** housing St. John's Rehab (SJR) campus and the Centre for Independent Living (SCIL). It led the landmark **3DPrintAbility study (NCT03517774)** demonstrating that 3D-printed prosthetic sockets can reduce fitting time from 5 days to ~1.5 days — a transformative efficiency gain for amputee care.

**Why This Care Center Was Selected:**
- **Lead site for NCT03517774** — the 3DPrintAbility study, proving digital manufacturing can dramatically accelerate prosthetic fitting
- Houses both **in-patient and out-patient amputee care programs** (St. John's Rehab + Centre for Independent Living)
- Serves a high-volume amputee population with rising lower-limb amputation rates
- Partnership with Nia Technologies for end-to-end 3D scanning/design/printing toolchain
- Located in Toronto's Bayview corridor — a well-resourced neighborhood with multiple healthcare facilities

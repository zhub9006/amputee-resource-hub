## 📊 Clinical Trial Findings: Affordable Prosthetic Devices

Based on a search of **6 verified recent studies** on ClinicalTrials.gov focused on affordable prosthetic devices and amputee rehabilitation, plus an aggregated analysis of **439 prosthetic/amputation studies** in the database.

### Key Studies

| NCT ID | Title | Sponsor | Status | Affordability Angle |
|--------|-------|---------|--------|---------------------|
| **NCT04725461** | Low Cost Socket for Lower Limb Amputees | Shirley Ryan AbilityLab | Completed | 3D-printed expandable foam socket; cuts fabrication from days to <1.5 hrs |
| **NCT04953364** | SMART: Self-Management for Amputee Rehabilitation | Univ of British Columbia | Recruiting | Online peer-supported mHealth rehab; reduces travel costs for rural amputees |
| **NCT04238533** | eADAPT for Myoelectric Prosthetic Users | Univ of South Florida | Unknown | Game-based app training; cheap, scalable, home-usable |
| **NCT04059107** | 3D Printed Myoelectric Prosthetics in Children | Oregon Health & Science Univ | Enrolling | 3D-printed bionic hands; dramatically lower cost than traditional |
| **NCT04700085** | ReMotion Prosthetic Knee (Reverse Innovation) | Sint Maartenskliniek | Completed | $80 mechanical knee vs $1,000-$4,000 standard; ISO 10328 approved |
| **NCT06883942** | Tripping Devices for Lower Limbs (Fall Risk) | Fondazione Don Carlo Gnocchi | Completed | Fall detection for amputees; safety of prosthetic systems |

### Phase Distribution (439 prosthetic/amputation studies)

| Phase | Count | % | Significance |
|-------|-------|---|--------------|
| **N/A** | 319 | 73% | Device/procedure studies — most common for prosthetic research |
| **Unknown** | 98 | 22% | Early-stage or not yet assigned |
| **Phase 3** | **5** | 1% | Definitive evidence for regulatory approval — critical unmet need |
| **Phase 2** | 10 | 2% | Early efficacy signals |
| **Phase 1** | 7 | 2% | Initial safety only |

> **Key takeaway:** Prosthetic research is overwhelmingly early-stage. Only 5 Phase 3 trials exist across 439 studies. Most individual studies are small-sample (5-82 participants).

### Sponsor Type Breakdown

| Type | Count | % | Significance |
|------|-------|---|--------------|
| **Academic (OTHER)** | 289 | 66% | Dominates innovation pipeline — leads affordability research |
| **Industry** | 91 | 21% | Growing partnership on affordable training tools and components |
| **Federal (VA, DoD)** | 48 | 11% | Strong veteran focus; critical prosthetics R&D funding |
| **Other Gov** | 9 | 2% | Government-sponsored health research |

> **Key takeaway:** 87% of prosthetic research is academic + industry. Federal sponsors provide critical veteran infrastructure. Only 5 Phase 3 studies exist — this gap is where academic-industry partnership matters most.

### Innovation Categories & Affordability

| Category | Study | Status | Affordability Angle |
|----------|-------|--------|---------------------|
| **3D-Printed Sockets** | NCT04725461 | Completed | <90 min fabrication vs. multi-day; digital manufacturing reduces cost |
| **mHealth Training** | NCT04238533 | Unknown | Cheap, scalable, home-usable app; cuts training cost |
| **Remote Rehab** | NCT04953364 | Recruiting | Online peer-supported rehab; eliminates travel barriers |
| **3D-Printed Prosthetics** | NCT04059107 | Enrolling | Dramatically lower cost than traditional myoelectric devices |
| **Reverse Innovation** | NCT04700085 | Completed | $80 knee vs $1,000-$4,000; ISO-certified regulatory pathway |

### Most Promising Directions

1. **3D-Printed Prosthetics & Sockets** — Lower-cost, faster fabrication; deployable in low-resource settings.
2. **mHealth & Remote Rehab** — Digital tools reduce cost and accessibility barriers for rural/underserved populations.
3. **Reverse Innovation** — Low-income country devices ($80) can work in high-income settings with ISO certification.

## 🏥 Care Center: Shirley Ryan AbilityLab

**Address:** 355 E Erie St, Streeterville, Chicago, IL 60611
**Ranked:** #1 Rehabilitation Hospital in the US (US News & World Report)
**Website:** shirleyryanabilitylab.org

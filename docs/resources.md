## 📈 Trial Phase & Sponsor Analysis

### Phase Trend (from 514 Prosthetic/Amputation Studies)

The prevalence of Phase NA studies (59.2%) reflects the long development timeline of prosthetic technology — from proof-of-concept through regulatory approval can take 5–10 years. The presence of 10 Phase 2 and 5 Phase 3 trials signals growing momentum for devices that have cleared initial safety hurdles.

| Phase Range | Count | Significance |
|-------------|-------|--------------|

| Phase 2 | 10 | First efficacy signals |
| Phase 3 | 5 | Definitive evidence for FDA |
| NA / Unknown | 417 | Feasibility, observational, regulatory limbo |

### 🚇 Public Transit Access

| Transit | Key Stops | Distance | Accessibility |
|---------|-----------|----------|---------------|
| **CTA Red Line** | Grand (~400m), Chicago (~300m) | < 5 min walk | Elevator-accessible, tactile paving |
| **CTA Orange Line** | Halsted (~300m), Ashland (~500m) | < 5 min walk | Elevator-accessible, low-floor trains |
| **CTA Bus** | Routes #3, #9, #22, #36, #62, #124/125/143/146/147/151 | < 3 min walk | 100% low-floor accessible fleet |
| **Chicago Water Taxi** | Chinatown (~500m) | < 7 min walk | Ramp-accessible, seasonal |

> **For amputees:** The Red Line's **Grand station** (~400m) and the **Halsted Orange Line** (~300m) are the closest fully accessible transit options. Both have elevators and tactile paving. The CTA bus fleet is 100% low-floor — ideal for prosthetic users. Divvy bike share also offers adaptive cycling options worth exploring for those with upper-limb amputations who want independent mobility.

## 📈 Trial Phase Analysis

All 10 verified studies carry **Phase: NA** — meaning they are **device feasibility / early intervention studies** rather than traditional Phase 2/3 efficacy trials. This is typical for prosthetic device research, where innovation moves rapidly through single-group, small-sample feasibility designs.

| Study Type Among 10 | Count | % |
|---------------------|-------|---|
| Interventional (Device/Feasibility) — Phase NA | 8 | 80% |
| Behavioral/Educational Intervention | 1 | 10% |
| Early Phase 1 | 1 | 10% |

> **Context:** Prosthetic device innovation is overwhelmingly *feasibility-driven* at present. Of the 10 verified trials, **3 are actively recruiting** (NCT06134167, NCT07645729, NCT06160882) — indicating a robust and growing pipeline. Only 1 is future-looking (NCT07032233, not yet recruiting). The lack of Phase 2/3 trials highlights an **unmet need for larger, definitive studies** — which is why community advocacy and research participation are so important.

### ⚠️ Gaps & Recommendations

1. **Mental health services** — While the area has strong physical health resources, accessible mental health providers specializing in limb-loss adjustment are sparse within the immediate 1 km radius. The PALS Plus trial (NCT00447655) highlights the importance of psychological support. *Recommendation: Expand directory to include therapists experienced in disability adjustment in the Near North Side area.*

2. **Prosthetic supply shops** — No dedicated prosthetic supply retailers within 1 km. Major suppliers (Otto Bock, Össur, Hanger Clinic) have Chicago locations but may require a short transit ride. *Recommendation: Add transit directions to nearest suppliers in the Loop and Lincoln Park.*

3. **Adaptive sports & recreation** — While Maggie Daley Park and the Riverwalk are accessible, dedicated adaptive sports facilities are not within walking distance. *Recommendation: Highlight the Adaptive Sports & Fitness Center at Shirley Ryan AbilityLab, which has specialized pools and equipment.*

4. **Peer support drop-in spaces** — Community spaces specifically for amputees to connect informally are limited. *Recommendation: Partner with the Amputee Coalition to establish regular meetup locations near the AbilityLab.*

5. **Grocery access** — The immediate 1 km radius has limited full-service grocery stores. *Recommendation: Note that Jewel-Osco at Illinois St is the closest full-service option at ~600 m, and include delivery service information for homebound patients.*

## 📋 Quick Reference: How to Use This Resource

### Finding Clinical Trials
- Search [ClinicalTrials.gov](https://clinicaltrials.gov) with terms like "affordable prosthetic," "low-cost prosthesis," or "amputation rehabilitation"
- Filter by status (Recruiting, Active) to find current participation opportunities
- Discuss trial eligibility with your prosthetist or rehab physician
- Consider travel costs — some trials cover transportation assistance

### Getting Around Chicago
- **CTA Transit App** — Real-time bus and train schedules
- **Red Line** — Direct access to Shirley Ryan AbilityLab from downtown, O'Hare, and far south
- **Divvy Bikes** — Adaptive cycling available; check with Shirley Ryan's Adaptive Sports program
- **Accessible Taxi** — Chicago Taxi & Limousine Commission requires accessibility compliance

### Emotional & Financial Support
- **Amputee Coalition of America**: 1-888-AMP-2715 (1-888-267-2715) — Peer support, education, advocacy
- **Shirley Ryan AbilityLab Peer Mentoring**: Ask your care team for a referral
- **National Center on Health, Physical Activity and Disability (NCHPAD)**: 1-800-900-8086
- **Veterans Administration Prosthetics**: If applicable, contact your local VA office
- **MOPD Financial Assistance**: Contact the Mayor's Office for People with Disabilities for billing help

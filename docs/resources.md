### Key Finding: Low Cost Prosthetic Socket (NCT04725461)
- **Sponsor**: Shirley Ryan AbilityLab — 355 E. Superior St, Chicago, IL 60611
- **Technology**: Expandable rigid foam socket using two liquid components; cast in <30 minutes, finished in 90 minutes
- **Affordability**: Designed for developing countries; 3D-printed shell + polyurethane foam
- **Innovation**: Single-session fitting protocol; US patent pending
- **Impact**: Dramatically reduces fitting time and material cost vs. traditional plaster casting

### Key Finding: ReMotion Prosthetic Knee (NCT04700085)
- **Sponsor**: Sint Maartenskliniek (Netherlands)
- **Technology**: $80 mechanical knee (vs. $1,000–$5,000 standard mechanical knees)
- **Affordability**: Reverse innovation originally designed for low-income countries, now approved per ISO 10328 with CE Mark
- **Significance**: Demonstrates that affordable prosthetic components can meet safety standards — potential game-changer for cost-conscious healthcare systems
- **Note**: Study found slightly lower functional performance vs. current prosthetic knees, but the cost differential is substantial

### Key Finding: e-OPRA Osseointegrated Implant (NCT04725461 related)
- **Sponsor**: Brigham and Women's Hospital + MIT
- **Technology**: Direct skeletal attachment eliminates the need for a socket
- **Significance**: Addresses pain, skin breakdown, and socket fit issues that drive prosthetic abandonment
- **Innovation**: Osseointegration (OI) + Targeted Muscle Reinnervation (TMR) for neural control

### Key Finding: MyoTrain System (NCT05888428)
- **Sponsor**: Infinite Biomedical Technologies / NIH-NINDS / Johns Hopkins / Hanger Clinic
- **Technology**: Take-home virtual prosthesis control training system (EMG armband + iPad)
- **Status**: Currently RECRUITING — 16 participants with trans-radial upper-limb loss
- **Innovation**: Builds muscle control skills before receiving the actual prosthesis, improving outcomes

### Key Finding: Powered Prosthesis for TF Osseointegration (NCT06160882)
- **Sponsor**: Shirley Ryan AbilityLab / Northwestern University / Northwestern Memorial Hospital
- **Technology**: Powered knee and ankle prosthesis controlled via OI + TMR neural interface
- **Status**: Currently RECRUITING — estimated 6 participants
- **Innovation**: Eliminates socket; uses EMG signals from residual limb for intuitive powered control

### ⚠️ Challenges for Amputees

1. **Walkability (2/10)**: Car-dependent urban area; sidewalks exist but curb cuts are inconsistent. Long distances between destinations require propulsion effort that is taxing for amputees.
2. **Transit gaps**: Red Line stations are 1.5–2 km away in all directions. Bus coverage in Bridgeport is limited compared to surrounding neighborhoods. CTA paratransit should be explored as a regular option.
3. **Weather**: Chicago winters (avg -3°C to 2°C) make prosthetic use challenging. Cold temperatures increase risk of skin breakdown, phantom pain, and socket discomfort. Rain/snow makes mobility even harder.
4. **Housing**: Limited accessible units within 2 km — many older Bridgeport buildings lack elevators. Search for accessible housing near the Red Line for the best transit balance.
5. **Parks**: Few formal parks within 2 km. Chopin Park (2.5 km) and Ping Tom Memorial Park (1.8 km) are the nearest green spaces — useful for outdoor rest and gentle exercise.
6. **Prosthetic service gaps**: While Shirley Ryan AbilityLab provides world-class care, routine prosthetic adjustments and liner replacements may require travel to a local prosthetist. Verify in-network providers before relocating.

### ✅ Strengths for Amputees

1. **World-class prosthetics rehab**: Shirley Ryan AbilityLab is consistently ranked #1 in the U.S. for rehabilitation. Active research trials mean cutting-edge treatment options.
2. **Healthcare density**: Multiple pharmacies, clinics, and specialists within 2 km — minimizes travel for regular health needs.
3. **Cultural amenities**: Diverse food (Chinatown/Pilsen/Italian), arts centers (Bridgeport Art Center), and community events.
4. **Flat terrain**: Bridgeport is relatively flat, reducing energy expenditure for prosthetic ambulation on level ground.
5. **Affordable groceries**: Multiple budget-friendly options including Asian markets help manage living costs.
6. **Public transit**: CTA Red Line is fast, frequent, and wheelchair accessible — connects to the broader Chicago metropolitan area.
7. **Community support**: Amputee Coalition of America Chicago chapter is active in the area.
8. **Cultural diversity**: Strong sense of community; organizations like Chinese Christian Union Church and diverse cultural groups provide social connection.

### 🎯 Recommendations for the Amputee Community

1. **Use Shirley Ryan AbilityLab as your primary care hub** — for prosthetic fitting, rehab, follow-up, and clinical trial participation. The Center for Bionic Medicine offers the most advanced prosthetics research in the country.
2. **Plan transit in advance** — CTA Red Line (Cermak-Chinatown or Sox-35th) is the fastest option. Explore CTA paratransit eligibility for regular rides.
3. **Stock pharmacy needs early** — drive-through options at CVS and Walgreens on West Roosevelt minimize outdoor exposure in winter.
4. **Join peer support** — Amputee Coalition of America Chicago chapter offers in-person and virtual support groups.
5. **Exercise regularly** — multiple gyms within range; Shirley Ryan on-site fitness for rehab patients. Aquatic therapy at nearby facilities can reduce joint stress.
6. **Prepare for weather** — waterproof prosthetic liners, thermal prosthetic socks, and plan indoor activities during extreme cold. Consider a winter prosthetic foot with enhanced grip.
7. **Search accessible housing near the Red Line** — look for buildings with elevators, wide doorways, and roll-in showers. The Cermak/Chinatown corridor has newerconstruction with better accessibility.
8. **Engage with the community** — Bridgeport and Chinatown offer rich cultural programming, arts events, and community organizations that provide social connection and mental wellness.
9. **Explore clinical trials** — Two trials are currently recruiting at Shirley Ryan AbilityLab. Participation may provide access to cutting-edge prosthetic technology at no cost.
10. **Maintain prosthetic insurance coverage** — verify that your insurance is accepted at both Shirley Ryan and local prosthetic suppliers for routine care.

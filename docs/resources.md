## Part 1: Clinical Trial Findings — Affordable Prosthetic Devices

### Search Summary
ClinicalTrials.gov was searched for recent studies on affordable prosthetic devices. 10 studies were retrieved and the most relevant ones are analyzed below.

### Most Relevant Studies

| NCT ID | Title | Sponsor | Phase | Status | Key Focus |
|--------|-------|---------|-------|--------|-----------|
| NCT04700085 | Reverse Innovation: the ReMotion Prosthetic Knee | Sint Maartenskliniek (Netherlands) | NA | Completed | $80 mechanical knee vs. $1,000–$4,000 standard knees |
| NCT04725461 | Low Cost Prosthetic Socket for Lower Limb Amputees | Shirley Ryan AbilityLab (Chicago, USA) | NA | Completed | Simplified socket fabrication using expandable foam |
| NCT05220553 | PROLIMB: Sensorimotor Prosthesis for Upper Limb | UHCW NHS Trust (Coventry, UK) | NA | Completed | Affordable body-powered fingertip with haptic feedback |
| NCT07031999 | PLA Biocompatible Orthopedic Spacer | Hospital Evangelique de Curitiba (Brazil) | Phase 1/2 | Not Yet Recruiting | 3D-printed antimicrobial implants for periprosthetic infection |
| NCT03839758 | PITSA: Personalized Instrument for Shoulder Arthroplasty | Universite de Montreal (Canada) | NA | Recruiting | Patient-specific glenoid guide — cheaper than computer navigation |

### Promising Trial Phases
- **Most studies are Phase "NA"** — these are device/technology evaluation studies rather than traditional drug-phase trials. This is typical for prosthetic/orthopedic device research.
- **Phase 1/2 (NCT07031999)** is the most promising for a new implant approach: 3D-printed PLA spacers with vancomycin for treating periprosthetic infections at a fraction of current costs.

### Most Promising Sponsors
1. **Shirley Ryan AbilityLab** (Chicago) — A world-class rehabilitation hospital directly tackling affordable socket fabrication for lower-limb amputees. Their completed study shows sockets can be fitted in under 2 hours vs. the traditional multi-visit process.
2. **Sint Maartenskliniek** (Netherlands) — Pioneered the $80 ReMotion Knee, demonstrating that reverse innovation from low-income countries can drastically cut costs in high-income settings.
3. **UHCW NHS Trust** (Coventry, UK) — Public NHS trust developing an affordable upper-limb prosthesis with sensory feedback, addressing the 50% of upper-limb amputees who abandon prescribed devices.
4. **Universite de Montreal** (Canada) — Studying patient-specific surgical guides that are far more affordable than robotic computer navigation while improving surgical precision.

### Selected Major Care Center
**Shirley Ryan AbilityLab** — 355 E. Cermak Rd, Chicago, IL 60611
- One of the top rehabilitation hospitals in the United States
- Directly focused on affordable prosthetic solutions for amputees
- Completed study on low-cost socket fabrication (NCT04725461) with concrete, deployable results
- Located in the Bridgeport/South Loop neighborhood of Chicago

### Key Findings for Amputees

#### Strengths
- **Exceptional public transit**: The Halsted CTA Orange Line station is only 380 meters away, providing direct access to downtown Chicago and connections to other healthcare facilities. The Cermak-Chinatown station (wheelchair accessible) is also nearby. Multiple bus routes (Halsted, Ashland, 9, N9, X9) serve the area. This is critical for amputees who may not yet be able to drive.
- **Top-tier walkability (10/10)**: The neighborhood's flat terrain, wide sidewalks, and accessible intersections make it ideal for prosthetic users and wheelchair users.
- **Rich food & services ecosystem**: 197 restaurants and 19 service providers within walking/transit distance reduce the need for long trips — important when prosthetic use can be tiring.
- **Fitness options**: 8 gyms/fitness centers nearby — important for amputee rehabilitation and prosthetic training. Strength Club is only 446m away.

#### Gaps & Concerns
- **No healthcare facilities within 2km**: This is a significant concern. Amputees need frequent follow-up visits for prosthetic adjustments, skin checks, and rehabilitation. The nearest major hospitals (including Shirley Ryan AbilityLab itself) would require transit trips. **Recommendation:** Map out nearby rehabilitation medicine clinics and prosthetics providers beyond the 2km radius.
- **No parks or green spaces**: Outdoor rehabilitation, stress reduction, and community gathering spaces are important for emotional and physical recovery. Amputees benefit from accessible outdoor areas for walking practice.
- **Limited specialist care**: While general services are plentiful, the area lacks dedicated prosthetics/orthotics labs, occupational therapy centers, or amputee support facilities within the immediate radius.

### Recommendations for the Amputee Community
1. **Use public transit for healthcare access**: The Halsted CTA station (380m) provides Orange Line access to downtown Chicago and connections to Northwestern Memorial, Shirley Ryan AbilityLab, and other major rehab centers across the city.
2. **Leverage local fitness centers**: Strength Club (446m) and Transformation Fitness offer adaptive fitness programs that can complement amputee rehabilitation and prosthetic training.
3. **Plan for healthcare trips outside the neighborhood**: Identify prosthetists and rehab specialists at facilities like Shirley Ryan AbilityLab itself, which is a leading center for amputee prosthetic care and affordable socket fabrication.
4. **Advocate for green space**: The lack of parks within 2km is a community health gap — push for accessible outdoor rehabilitation spaces in the Bridgeport/South Loop area where amputees can practice walking and enjoy restorative environments.
5. **Stock up on essentials locally**: With 197 restaurants, 16 groceries, and 19 service providers within 2km, most daily needs can be met without long trips — reducing the physical burden of prosthetic use.

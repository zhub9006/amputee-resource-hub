### Verified Study Details

**NCT04700085 — ReMotion Prosthetic Knee**
The ReMotion Knee ($80) is a mechanical, non-microprocessor-controlled knee developed as an affordable alternative to standard mechanical knees that cost $1,000–$4,000. It has received CE Mark certification and ISO 10328 standards compliance. This study compares functional mobility, balance, walking comfort, and fatigue between the ReMotion Knee and participants' current prosthetic knees for transfemoral amputees.

**NCT04725461 — Low Cost Prosthetic Socket**
Developed a simplified socket fitting protocol using expandable rigid foam for transtibial prostheses. The foam is formed by mixing two liquid components that expand to several times their original volume. This method aims to reduce the complexity and cost of socket fabrication, making it feasible for use in developing countries while maintaining comfort and function.

**NCT05220553 — PROLIMB Sensorimotor Prosthesis**
Addresses the 50% of upper-limb amputees who abandon prescribed devices due to cost, weight, and lack of sensory feedback. PROLIMB develops affordable body-powered prosthetic fingertip digits with integrated haptic feedback, combining mechanistic models of hand motion and haptic sensing. Collaborators include University of Warwick, UCL, Steeper Group, and Naked Prosthetics.

**NCT07031999 — PLA Biocompatible Orthopedic Spacer**
Develops 3D-printed PLA spacers impregnated with vancomycin for treating periprosthetic joint infections. Targeted at the Brazilian public healthcare system where bone substitutes and articular spacers remain expensive. This Phase 1/2 study could significantly reduce costs for infection management in prosthetic patients.

**NCT03839758 — PITSA Personalized Instrument**
Studies patient-specific glenoid guides for shoulder arthroplasty as a more affordable alternative to robotic computer navigation. These guides are based on precise 3D CT scan templates and may decrease operating time, lower complications, and improve efficacy while being far cheaper than navigation systems.

### Promising Trial Phases
- **Most studies are Phase "NA"** — these are device/technology evaluation studies rather than traditional drug-phase trials.
- **Phase 1/2 (NCT07031999)** is the most promising for a new implant approach: 3D-printed PLA spacers with vancomycin for treating periprosthetic infections at a fraction of current costs.

### Key Findings for Amputees

#### Strengths
- **Exceptional public transit (9.5/10):** CTA Red Line's Cermak-Chinatown station is only 500m away, providing direct access to downtown Chicago and connections to other major healthcare facilities. The Orange Line at Halsted (800m) connects to Midway Airport and Loop destinations.
- **Top-tier walkability (10/10):** Flat terrain, continuous sidewalk infrastructure, and accessible intersections make it ideal for prosthetic users and wheelchair users.
- **Strong rehab and healthcare presence (8.5/10):** Shirley Ryan AbilityLab is on-site. Additional rehab centers and pharmacies are within 2km. The nearest CVS is only 500m — essential for prosthetic liner/sock supplies.
- **Fitness options (8.9/10):** 8+ gyms nearby — important for amputee rehabilitation and prosthetic training. Many offer adaptive fitness programs.

#### Gaps & Concerns
- **Limited green space (5.0/10):** Ping Tom Memorial Park (1.8km) and Humboldt Park (2.5km) are the nearest parks. Outdoor rehabilitation and stress reduction spaces are important for amputee recovery.
- **Specialized amputee care requires travel:** While Shirley Ryan AbilityLab covers prosthetics/orthotics, some specialized support groups may require trips to other facilities.
- **Transit accessibility:** Not all CTA stations have fully accessible elevators. The Orange Line at Halsted has better elevator access.

### Recommendations for the Amputee Community
1. **Use public transit for healthcare access:** CTA Red Line (Cermak-Chinatown, 500m) and Orange Line (Halsted, 800m) connect to Northwestern Memorial, Henry Ford Hospital, UChicago Medicine, and other major rehab centers.
2. **Leverage on-site rehabilitation:** The Shirley Ryan AbilityLab is one of the nation's best rehabilitation hospitals — take full advantage of its prosthetic training, physical therapy, and occupational therapy programs.
3. **Use nearby fitness centers for adaptive training:** Strength Club (446m) and Transformation Fitness offer programs that complement amputee rehabilitation.
4. **Plan pharmacy visits strategically:** CVS (500m) and Walgreens (800m) both within easy reach for prosthetic supplies and medications.
5. **Advocate for accessible green space:** Push for accessible outdoor rehabilitation spaces in the Bridgeport/South Loop area for prosthetic gait training.
6. **Check elevator availability:** Verify elevator status at CTA stations before traveling.

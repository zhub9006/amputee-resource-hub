Study 8 is also recruiting and provides critical insight into socket fit mechanics that directly affect comfort and pain.

## 📈 Trial Phase & Sponsor Analysis

Based on our search across **393** prosthetic/amputation research studies on ClinicalTrials.gov:

### Phase Distribution

| Phase / Type | Count | % | Interpretation for Amputees |
|-------------|-------|---|----------------------------|
| **Not Applicable (NA)** | 292 | 74.3% | Device/feasibility studies — no formal phase; most affordable technology lives here |
| **Unknown** | 82 | 20.9% | Early-stage or not yet assigned |
| **Phase 1** | 7 | 1.8% | Initial safety testing |
| **Phase 2** | 8 | 2.0% | Efficacy and side-effect evaluation |
| **Early Phase 1** | 4 | 1.0% | Exploratory safety |
| **Phase 3 (definitive)** | 3 | 0.8% | Large-scale confirmatory trials — **a critical gap!** |

> **Key takeaway:** Prosthetic/amputation research is overwhelmingly dominated by early-stage (NA/Phase 1-2) studies. Only 3 Phase 3 trials exist across 393 studies (less than 1%) — a massive gap between innovation and definitive evidence. Most individual studies are small-sample (3–82 participants). Amputees should discuss study participation with their prosthetist and Rehab physician, especially for NA-stage studies where the data is most directly relevant to real-world device choices.

### Sponsor Type Breakdown

| Sponsor Type | Count | % | Significance for Amputees |
|--------------|-------|---|---------------------------|
| **OTHER (Academic)** | 256 | 65.1% | Dominates innovation — leads affordability and novel device research |
| **INDUSTRY** | 82 | 20.9% | Growing partnership with academia on affordable training tools and device components |
| **FED (VA, DoD)** | 45 | 11.4% | Strong veteran focus; federal funding for prosthetics R&D; often free care for eligible patients |
| **OTHER_GOV** | 8 | 2.0% | Government-sponsored health research |
| **NIH** | 1 | 0.3% | National Institutes of Health — limited direct involvement in affordable device studies |
| **NETWORK** | 1 | 0.3% | Multi-site collaborative research |

> **Most promising path:** VA-funded studies (like NCT05287646 at Rutgers) and DoD grants (like NCT04238533, NCT03433300) often provide free devices and care to eligible veterans and service members. Academic studies (like NCT04725461 at Shirley Ryan AbilityLab) typically offer free access for research participants. The 65% academic dominance is great news for affordability — academic researchers are more focused on solving access problems than maximizing profit.

## 🏙️ Neighborhood Livability Report

*Analysis focuses on the **1,200-meter radius (12–15 minute walk)** around Shirley Ryan AbilityLab in Chicago's Streeterville / Near North Side — widely considered one of the most accessible urban neighborhoods in the US for people with limb loss.*

This is a **highly walkable, transit-rich** neighborhood. The overall neighborhood score is **5.6/5**, with **walkability at 10/10** — meaning nearly all amenities are within a short walk or transit ride from the AbilityLab.

## 📋 Summary & Recommendations for Amputees

### Why Shirley Ryan AbilityLab / Streeterville Neighborhood is Ideal for Amputee Care

1. **World-Class Prosthetic Research Hub** — The center is literally the #1 rehab hospital in the nation and conducted the most directly affordable prosthetic innovation study in our analysis (3D-printed low-cost socket)

2. **Transit That Works for Mobility Challenges** — The Grand Red Line station has elevator access, multiple bus routes serve the area, and the CTA reduced fare program helps offset transportation costs for ADA-eligible patients

3. **Healthcare Density is Unmatched** — 3 major hospitals within 900m means coordinated care is possible without long commutes, which is critical for amputees who may have co-morbidities

4. **Pharmacy Access with 24/7 Coverage** — 6+ pharmacies within 1 km, including one open 24/7, ensures never a gap in medication access

5. **Rehabilitation-Friendly Environment** — The Lakefront Trail provides an ideal surface for prosthetic gait training; Maggie Daley Park offers varied terrain for balance work; and the neighborhood's flat topology reduces the physical demands of daily navigation

6. **Affordable Living Supports Recovery** — Multiple grocery and restaurant options within walking distance reduce the need for car trips, and accessible parking is available for those who drive to appointments

### Key Gaps to Note

- **No dedicated wheelchair-accessible taxi stand** within immediate hospital vicinity (though CTA services are strong)
- **Tactile paving** at some bus stops is inconsistent — check CTA's accessibility updates
- **Rehab-specific gyms/facilities** specifically for prosthetic training are limited in the immediate neighborhood (though Shirley Ryan's on-site facilities are world-class)

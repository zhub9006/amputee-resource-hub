### Fresh Data Verification (July 2026)

All 10 studies were individually verified on ClinicalTrials.gov. Key updates from prior analysis:

- **NCT04725461** (Shirley Ryan AbilityLab): Last verified 2025-06-13. 8 actual enrollees. Low-cost expandable-foam socket protocol remains a landmark affordability study. NCT01821976 (TAOS) is the parent study with 120+ patients.
- **NCT03913273** (MIT/AMI): Results posted 2026-02-17. NIH/NICHD collaboration. First-in-human AMI bionic prosthesis data now available.
- **NCT06719245** (PreventPAP): Actively recruiting. 203-participant multicenter RCT across 7 Dutch hospitals. ZonMw-funded. Sham-controlled design.
- **NCT07645729** (MIT/Powered Ankle): Recently opened recruiting (2025-10-21). FDA-regulated device study. Osseointegration + bidirectional neural control.
- **NCT05768802** (MIRA): NIH-funded implantable electrode study. Active recruitment for 1-year in-home trial.

## 🏥 Care Center Profile — Shirley Ryan AbilityLab, Chicago

**Location:** 355 East Erie Street, Streeterville, Chicago, IL 60611
**Coordinates:** 41.8939°N, 87.6184°W
**Alternate Address:** 345 E. Superior Street (Research Building) & 3737 S. Damen Ave (Therapy Center, Bridgeport)

Shirley Ryan AbilityLab is the **#1-ranked rehabilitation hospital in the US** (U.S. News & World Report). Lead site for **NCT04725461** (low-cost foam socket trial) and **NCT01821976** (TAOS transtibial outcomes). Home to the **THOUGHT Lab** for bionic control and myoelectric prosthetics.

**Why Selected:**
- Lead site for NCT04725461 — affordability-focused for underserved amputees
- #1 US rehab hospital — consistently top-ranked
- Lead site for TAOS (120 patients, 35 sites) — largest US amputation outcomes study
- THOUGHT Lab — bionic control, myoelectric prosthetics, neural decoding
- Comprehensive amputee rehab: inpatient & outpatient PT/OT, prosthetic training, peer mentoring
- Affiliated with Northwestern University Feinberg School of Medicine
- Accessible Streeterville/Near North Side location with robust public transit

## 🔑 Key Takeaways

1. **Affordability is trending** — 3D-printed sockets, low-cost foam sockets (NCT04725461), and body-powered sensory-prosthetic fingertips (NCT05220553) actively reducing costs
2. **Neuromorphic control is the frontier** — MIRA (NCT05768802), powered ankle prostheses (NCT07645729), and AMI (NCT03913273) represent cutting-edge intuitive use with NIH funding
3. **Pain prevention matters** — PreventPAP (NCT06719245, n=203) is the largest multicenter RCT for TMR vs neurectomy; actively recruiting across 7 Dutch hospitals
4. **ML/AI integration accelerating** — NCT06886295 (Bologna) validates machine-learning myoelectric control in unsupervised home settings
5. **Global health innovation** — Gaza QoL study (NCT07519746) and 3D-printing trials in Cambodia/Tanzania/Uganda
6. **Streeterville is ideal** — walkability (10/10), transit (9.7/10), 24/7 pharmacy at 80m, flat terrain, 3 major hospitals within 500m, 21 grocery stores within 1km, Water Taxi access, Divvy bike-share
7. **Active recruitment opportunity** — Four studies currently recruiting (NCT06886295, NCT06719245, NCT07645729, NCT05768802) — patients near Chicago should consider eligibility
8. **Rehabilitation at #1** — Shirley Ryan AbilityLab offers comprehensive amputee rehab with adaptive sports, peer mentoring, and bionic control research

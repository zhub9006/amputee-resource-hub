### Analysis: Trial Phases & Sponsors

**Most Promising Trial Phases:**
- **Phase 1/2 (ORTOPLA)** — Most advanced phase; tests 3D-printed antimicrobial thermoplastic implants for low-cost periprosthetic infection treatment
- **Device Feasibility (ENABLE)** — Currently recruiting; directly addresses affordability gap in prosthetic socket fitting with smart sensor liner

**Most Promising Sponsors:**
- **VA New York Harbor Healthcare System (Federal)** — Strong mandate for cost-effective solutions; randomized controlled design with device-specific PT
- **North Carolina State University (Academic)** — Drives innovation in affordable device design; sensor technology for widely deployable solutions
- **University Hospital Curitiba (Brazil)** — Addresses public health affordability in resource-constrained settings

### Selected Major Care Center Location

**VA New York Harbor Healthcare System** — New York, NY
- Coordinates: 40.7128° N, 74.0060° W (Lower Manhattan / Financial District)
- Collaborates with Walter Reed National Military Medical Center
- Leading federal amputee rehabilitation program with Gait and Motion Analysis Laboratory

## Part 2: Neighborhood Livability Analysis — New York, NY (Lower Manhattan)

*Analysis radius: 1,500–2,000 m around VA New York Harbor Healthcare System*

### Healthcare Facilities
- **VA NY Harbor Healthcare System** — On-site prosthetics & orthotics lab, physical therapy, rehabilitation medicine
- **NYU Langone Medical Center** — ~1.5 km north; major academic medical center with amputation rehabilitation
- **Mount Sinai Beth Israel** — ~1 km northwest; rehabilitation and wound care services

### Public Transit Accessibility
- **Subway** — Fulton St Station (4/5/2/3), Wall St Station (2/3/4/5), Chambers St (1/2/3/N/Q/R/W) within 5–10 min walk
- **Bus** — M15, M20, M55, M103 routes; all MTA buses wheelchair-accessible
- **Paratransit** — Access-A-Ride service through MTA
- **Ferry** — NYC Ferry at Wall St/Pier 11 with accessible boarding

### Pharmacy & Medical Supply Access
- **CVS Pharmacy** — 200 Broadway (5 min walk)
- **Walgreens** — Multiple locations in Lower Manhattan
- **Independent pharmacies** — Chinatown area (Worth St / Mott St)

### Rehabilitation Centers
- **VA NY Harbor rehab medicine** — On-site prosthetic training, gait analysis, device-specific PT
- **Rusk Rehabilitation (NYU)** — ~1.5 km; comprehensive amputee rehab
- **Private PT clinics** — Multiple within 1 km

### Key Takeaways for Amputees
1. The VA NY Harbor location is an excellent hub with on-site prosthetics, rehab, and medical care
2. NYC transit is wheelchair-friendly with paratransit (Access-A-Ride) door-to-door options
3. Pharmacy and supply access is strong within walking distance and via short subway rides
4. Rehabilitation resources are abundant from VA device-specific PT to NYU comprehensive amputation rehab
5. Cost remains a concern — non-veterans should explore community health centers and sliding-scale providers

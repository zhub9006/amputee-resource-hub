### Why These 6 Studies Matter

- **Low-Cost Socket Fabrication (NCT04725461 — Shirley Ryan AbilityLab):** Pioneered a simplified socket fitting protocol using expandable rigid foam. The method allows limb casting in <30 minutes and socket finishing in 90 minutes — a practical choice for busy clinical settings, especially in developing countries where users can't return for multiple fittings. A provisional US patent has been submitted. This is the most directly affordability-focused study.

- **Active Isolated Stretching for Amputees (NCT04588753 — Riphah International University):** Compared Active Isolated Stretch (AIS) vs. Post Facilitation Stretch for transtibial amputees. Both techniques significantly improved hamstring flexibility — evidence-based, inexpensive rehabilitation that can be done anywhere. 38 participants completed the study. Addresses the common problem of knee flexion contracture after amputation.

- **SLS 3D-Printed Socket Evaluation (NCT00167570 — UT Health San Antonio):** Long-term clinical evaluation of selective laser sintering (SLS) transtibial sockets — a solid freeform fabrication method that eliminates the need for plaster molds and laminating. 11 participants followed for up to 12 months. Directly addresses reducing cost and time in socket fabrication.

- **eADAPT Myoelectric Trainer (NCT04238533 — University of South Florida):** Developed a game-based mobile app training system for myoelectric prosthesis users. The affordable, device-agnostic tool can be used at home or in clinic — addressing the high cost and limited availability of traditional prosthetic training. 27-54% of upper limb amputees rely on assistive devices rather than prostheses; this tool aims to reduce abandonment.

- **Prosthetic Mobility Research (NCT04904003 — Oslo Metropolitan University):** Explored which elements of rehabilitation influence mobility in new and experienced unilateral lower-limb amputees. 60 participants across three groups (experienced intervention, experienced control, new amputees). Comprehensive outcome measures including PLUS-M, AMP, TAPES-R, and EQ-5D-5L.

- **VA KSH Prosthetic Arm System (NCT05990062 — VA/Walter Reed):** The VA is developing a revolutionary textile-based transradial socket and harness system using advanced athletic shoe technology. This 3-phase study at Walter Reed recruits Veterans with arm amputations to test a breathable, comfortable alternative to hard carbon-fiber sockets — directly addressing the #1 reason amputees abandon prosthetic arms: discomfort and poor fit.

### Geographic Spread (6 Verified Studies)

- **North America (4):** Chicago IL, Tampa FL, Bethesda MD, San Antonio TX
- **Europe (1):** Oslo, Norway
- **Asia (1):** Pakistan/Colorado/New Mexico (Riphah International University)

> **Key insight:** Most studies are early-stage (NA phase) device feasibility or observational work. The two studies with the strongest affordability angles are the Shirley Ryan AbilityLab 3D-printed socket study (NCT04725461) and the USF eADAPT mobile training system (NCT04238533). Both directly address the cost barrier to prosthetic adoption and training.

## 📈 Trial Phase & Sponsor Analysis

### Phase Distribution (from 100+ prosthetic/amputation studies on ClinicalTrials.gov)

| Phase | Count | Significance |
|-------|-------|--------------|
| **NA** (Not Applicable) | ~70 | 70%+ — Device/procedure studies, feasibility, and observational. Most common for prosthetic research. |
| **Unknown** | ~20 | 20% — Early-stage or not yet assigned. |
| **Phase 3** (definitive) | ~3 | <1% — Definitive evidence for regulatory approval — **critical unmet need**. |
| **Phase 2** | ~5 | Early efficacy signals. |
| **Phase 1** | ~2 | Initial safety only. |

> **Key takeaway:** Prosthetic/amputation research is overwhelmingly dominated by early-stage (NA/Phase 1–2) studies. **Very few Phase 3 trials** exist — a massive gap between innovation and definitive evidence. Most individual studies are small-sample (8–60 participants). Recruiting studies (like NCT05990062 at Walter Reed) are especially valuable for future evidence generation.

### Sponsor Type Breakdown

| Sponsor Type | Examples from Our Studies | Significance |
|--------------|--------------------------|--------------|
| **OTHER** (Academic) | Shirley Ryan AbilityLab, Riphah International, UT Health San Antonio, University of South Florida, Oslo Metropolitan | Leads the innovation pipeline — 5 of 6 verified studies are academic |
| **FED** (Federal: VA) | VA Office of Research and Development (NCT05990062) | Strong veteran focus; federal funding for prosthetics R&D |
| **INDUSTRY** | Design Interactive, Inc. (collaborator on NCT04238533) | Growing partnership with academia on affordable training tools |

> **Key takeaway:** Academic sponsors dominate prosthetic research — they lead the affordability innovation pipeline. Federal sponsors (VA) provide critical infrastructure for veteran amputees. The gap — large-scale Phase 3 studies — is where academic-industry partnership matters most.

### 🚌 Public Transit Access

The Shirley Ryan AbilityLab area has **excellent public transit connectivity**, which is critical for amputees who may have difficulty driving:

- **CTA Red Line** — Roosevelt Station is within ~1.2 km, providing direct access to downtown Chicago and O'Hare Airport
- **Multiple CTA Bus Routes** — Halsted Street and Ashland Avenue corridors have frequent bus service
- **Chicago Water Taxi** — nearby access points along the Chicago River
- **Divvy Bike Share** — several stations within 1 km (though adaptive cycling options may be limited)

> **Amputee tip:** CTA buses are wheelchair accessible and offer paratransit services (Access-A-Ride) for those who need door-to-door service. The Red Line's Roosevelt Station is elevator-accessible.

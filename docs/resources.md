### 🔬 Fresh ClinicalTrials.gov Data — Detailed Study Summaries

#### Study 1: PROLIMB — A Sensorimotor Prosthesis for the Upper Limb (NCT05220553)
- **Sponsor**: University Hospitals Coventry and Warwickshire NHS Trust (UK)
- **Collaborators**: University of Warwick, University College London
- **Status**: COMPLETED (July 2023 – August 2024)
- **Phase**: NA (Device)
- **Key Innovation**: Body-powered prosthetic fingertip digit with integrated mechanical haptic feedback. Uses a Leap Motion Controller for grasp adaptation research and a haptic sensor system that relays fine sensory information from the prosthetic fingertip to an actuator on the residual limb.
- **Affordability Angle**: Designed for developing countries; addresses the WHO-identified gap where only 2% of upper limb amputees worldwide have access to prosthetic care. Targets the ~£60M/year NHS spend on upper limb services.
- **Significance**: Demonstrates that affordable, body-powered prostheses with sensory feedback are feasible — a major step toward reducing dependence on expensive myoelectric devices (£20K–£75K).

#### Study 2: MDORP — Mobile Device Outcomes-based Rehabilitation Program (NCT02891707)
- **Sponsor**: Henry M. Jackson Foundation for the Advancement of Military Medicine
- **Collaborators**: Walter Reed National Military Medical Center, Uniformed Services University, University of Miami, Miami VA Healthcare System
- **Status**: ACTIVE_NOT_RECRUITING (May 2016 – May 2020)
- **Phase**: NA
- **Enrollment**: 150 participants (65 at Walter Reed, 65 at Miami VA)
- **Key Innovation**: Web-based mobile computing platform (ReLOAD system) using instrumented insoles with accelerometers, gyroscopes, and geomagnetic sensors to capture gait data and transmit it to clinicians for remote rehabilitation guidance.
- **Affordability Angle**: Explicitly designed to decrease cost to the healthcare system and reduce burden for service members/veterans with lower limb loss. Enables continuity of care from DoD/VA facilities to home.
- **Measured Outcomes**: Timed Up and Go, Six-minute Walk Test, Amputee Mobility Predictor (AMP/BAMP), Comprehensive High-level Activity Mobility Predictor (CHAMP), gait symmetry metrics.
- **Significance**: Validates remote rehab as a cost-effective model — reduces travel burden and clinic visits for amputees, directly addressing affordability of ongoing care.

### Phase & Sponsor Analysis

**Trial Phases:**
- All identified prosthetic device studies are categorized as **Phase NA** (Not Applicable). This is standard for device/procedural intervention studies, which follow rigorous medical device evaluation protocols (ISO 14155, FDA 21 CFR Part 812) rather than traditional drug trial phases.
- The most promising **ongoing/recruiting** studies are:
  - **MyoTrain System** (NCT05888428) — Industry + NIH academic collaboration; currently enrolling
  - **Powered Prosthesis with OI** (NCT06160882) — Academic medical center + university; currently recruiting
  - **Novel Adaptable Prosthetic Foot** (NCT06214026) — University + DoD partnership; currently recruiting

**Sponsor Types:**
- **Academic Medical Centers** (most promising for affordable innovation): Shirley Ryan AbilityLab, Brigham & Women's Hospital, Duke University, University of Washington, VA New York Harbor
- **Industry Partners**: Infinite Biomedical Technologies, Hanger Clinic, Ohio Willow Wood, Naked Prosthetics
- **Government/DoD**: U.S. Department of Defense (co-sponsor with University of Washington), VA system
- **International**: Sint Maartenskliniek (Netherlands), McGill University (Canada), University of Warwick (UK)

**Recommendation**: Academic medical center + NIH/DoD partnerships show the most promise for affordable, evidence-based prosthetic innovation. These sponsors prioritize accessibility, publish openly, and often have patient assistance programs. The FDA CDRH (Center for Devices and Radiological Health) also provides a clearer regulatory pathway for low-cost prosthetic devices compared to drug trials.

## Part 4: Key Takeaways for Amputees & Caregivers

### From Clinical Trial Analysis
1. **Affordable is emerging**: The ReMotion $80 knee and Shirley Ryan's $80 socket prove that low-cost prosthetic components can meet safety standards — this trend is accelerating.
2. **Academic + NIH/DoD partnerships** are the most promising pathway for affordable innovation — these sponsors publish openly and often have patient assistance programs.
3. **Remote rehabilitation** (MDORP study) is validated as a cost-reducing model — reduces travel burden and clinic visits for amputees.
4. **Osseointegration** (e-OPRA) may eliminate the need for sockets entirely — addressing the #1 reason amputees abandon prosthetics (pain, skin breakdown, poor fit).

### From the Bridgeport Neighborhood Analysis
1. **Healthcare is strong** (6.9/10): Shirley Ryan AbilityLab is world-class and anchors the area. Multiple pharmacies and PT clinics within walking/transit distance.
2. **Transit is workable** but requires planning: The Red Line is your best friend — three accessible stations within 2.3km. Use CTA paratransit in winter.
3. **Parks and green space is the biggest gap** (1.0/10): Plan your outdoor rehab around the Riverwalk and Ping Tom Memorial Park rather than expecting neighborhood green space.
4. **Fitness options are excellent** (8.9/10): The Shirley Ryan on-site fitness center is the gold standard for amputee exercise.
5. **Affordable living**: Bridgeport has diverse, affordable grocery options and a strong sense of community — the Amputee Coalition Chicago chapter is active here.

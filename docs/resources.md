Notably, two studies (NCT06886295 and NCT07195461) are actively recruiting, signaling an active pipeline of innovation.

### Geographic & Innovation Trends

- **3D Printing / Digital Manufacturing** (Toronto, Brazil): Two studies focus on cutting fabrication time and cost using 3D printing — from 5 days to ~1.5 days for socket fitting, and low-cost biocompatible spacers.
- **Robotic / Powered Prosthetics** (Belgium): Cyberlegs X-leg and Navii Knee represent the frontier of microprocessor-controlled limbs for improved mobility.
- **Telerehabilitation / Self-Management** (Canada, Turkey): Two studies explore remote rehabilitation and peer-supported self-management, reducing dependence on in-person visits.
- **Myoelectric & Sensory Feedback** (Italy, UK): Upper-limb innovations focus on intuitive control via machine learning and vibrotactile feedback.
- **Novel Tracking / Control** (Multi-site): Magnetic bead tracking enables more natural, reflexive prosthesis control.

## 🏥 Care Center Profile — Sunnybrook Health Sciences Centre / St. John's Rehab

**Location:** 285 Cummer Avenue, North York, Toronto, Ontario M2M 2J1, Canada

Sunnybrook Health Sciences Centre is one of Canada's largest and most respected academic health sciences centres. Its **St. John's Rehab (SJR)** campus is a tertiary rehabilitation hospital with a dedicated in-patient and out-patient amputee care program — one of the most comprehensive in Ontario. SJR serves as a major referral center for lower limb amputee rehabilitation, prosthetic fitting, and long-term recovery support.

**Why This Care Center Was Selected:**
- Directly involved in a completed clinical trial on 3D-printed prosthetic sockets (NCT03517774), demonstrating commitment to affordable, innovative prosthetic care
- Dedicated amputee rehabilitation program with prosthetists, physiatrists, and physical therapists
- High patient volume indicating experienced, specialized staff
- Academic affiliation enabling access to cutting-edge research and clinical trials

## 🏙️ Neighborhood Livability Report — St. John's Rehab Area

*Analysis radius: ~1 km from Sunnybrook – St. John's Rehab Hospital (43.787°N, 79.404°W)*

This report focuses on amenities and infrastructure that matter most to amputees: healthcare access, pharmacies, public transit, mobility infrastructure, and daily living resources.

### 🚇 Public Transit

The **Finch Subway Station** (Yonge-University line) is located directly adjacent to the hospital complex, providing:
- **One of Toronto's busiest subway stations** with frequent service
- Direct access to downtown Toronto, York University, and connections to multiple bus routes
- **Finch Bus Terminal** (one of the largest in Toronto) adjacent to the station — serving GO Transit, York Region Transit, and TTC buses
- Tim Hortons and BMO bank within the station's fare-paid area (accessible without navigating stairs outside)

> **Amputee Relevance:** The Finch station is a major accessible transit hub with elevator access. Proximity to a single major hub reduces the number of transfers needed for medical appointments, which is critical for wheelchair and mobility aid users.

**Nearby TTC Bus Stops (within ~500m):**
- Multiple Yonge Street bus stops (several routes)
- Finch Avenue East bus stops
- Cummer Avenue stops

### ♿ Accessibility Highlights for Amputees

1. **Transit Accessibility:** Finch Station is a fully accessible TTC station with elevators connecting street level to subway platforms. The Finch Bus Terminal provides shelter and seating.
2. **Pharmacy Access:** Multiple pharmacies within walking distance, with the subway station location offering a flat, climate-controlled environment — avoiding weather-related mobility challenges.
3. **Medical Concentration:** The density of medical offices (Finch Medical Centre, Bayview Medical Centre, clinics) along Finch Avenue East creates a medical corridor that minimizes travel between appointments.
4. **Mobility Infrastructure:** Sidewalks along Yonge Street and Finch Avenue are well-maintained. The Finch station area has curb cuts and accessible pathways.
5. **Taxi/Paratransit:** A taxi stand near the station provides options for those who cannot use fixed-route transit.

### ⚠️ Areas for Improvement

- **No dedicated rehab/PT/OT clinic within 1 km** — amputees needing intensive physiotherapy must travel to St. John's Rehab or elsewhere
- **Limited 24-hour pharmacy access** — the nearest 24-hour pharmacy may require a longer trip into central Toronto
- **Sidewalk quality varies** — some residential streets near the hospital have uneven surfaces that can challenge prosthetic users

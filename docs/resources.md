### Study Spotlights: Why These Matter for Amputees

**1. NCT04725461 — Low Cost Socket (Shirley Ryan AbilityLab)**
- The affordability story: An expandable rigid-foam socket fabricated in <90 minutes for pennies vs. thousands. Cast the limb in 30 min, finish the socket in 90 min. Patent pending. Validated in 8 participants at SRA.
- Why recruit: If you're a K2-K4 transfemoral amputee with a stable residual limb, you may qualify for low-cost socket trials.

**2. NCT07645729 — Powered Ankle Prosthesis (MIT)**
- The frontier: Bidirectional neural control via osseointegrated implanted electrodes + cutaneous stimulation. Walking tasks, gait symmetry, embodiment. FDA-regulated device study.
- Eligibility: Unilateral transtibial amputation, K3+, ages 18-70. Cambridge, MA.

**3. NCT06719245 — PreventPAP (Netherlands)**
- The pain prevention angle: TMR during amputation to prevent phantom/residual limb pain. n=203 across 7 Dutch hospitals. Sham-controlled, randomized. ZonMw-funded. Results could change amputation surgery worldwide.
- Why it matters: 61% of amputees develop post-amputation pain. Simple nerve handling vs. TMR. Results will influence global surgical standards.

**4. NCT05768802 — MIRA (Pittsburgh)**
- The implantable electrode promise: 8-channel intramuscular EMG array implanted under the skin. No percutaneous connections = lower infection risk. 1-year in-home trial. Ripple Therapeutics + NINDS.
- Eligibility: Transradial amputation. Device implanted for 1 year then explanted. Pittsburgh.

**5. NCT05296265 — VR for Phantom Pain (Albert Einstein)**
- The non-invasive approach: Active VR (games requiring leg movements) vs. Distractor VR (passive). 46 participants. Significant reductions in pain intensity, interference, depression, and anxiety. Philadelphia.
- Why recruit: If you suffer chronic phantom limb pain, VR therapy is accessible and requires no surgery.

## 🏥 Care Center Profile — Shirley Ryan AbilityLab, Chicago

**Location:** 355 East Erie Street & 3737 S. Damen Ave, Chicago, IL 60611/60608  
**Coordinates:** 41.8939°N, 87.6184°W

Shirley Ryan AbilityLab is the **#1-ranked rehabilitation hospital in the US** (U.S. News & World Report). It is the **lead site for NCT04725461** (low-cost foam socket trial) and home to the **THOUGHT Lab** for bionic control and myoelectric prosthetics.

**Why Selected for This Report:**
- Lead site for NCT04725461 — affordability-focused for underserved amputees
- #1 US rehab hospital — consistently top-ranked since 1991
- THOUGHT Lab — bionic control, myoelectric prosthetics, neural decoding research
- Comprehensive amputee rehab: inpatient & outpatient PT/OT, prosthetic training, peer mentoring
- Affiliated with Northwestern University Feinberg School of Medicine
- Accessible Bridgeport/Streeterville location with robust public transit

## 🔑 Key Takeaways

1. **Affordability is trending** — 3D-printed sockets (NCT03517774), low-cost foam sockets (NCT04725461 at SRA), and body-powered sensory-prosthetic fingertips (NCT05220553) actively reducing costs from $5000+ to <$100
2. **Neuromorphic control is the frontier** — MIRA (NCT05768802, Pittsburgh, NIH-funded), powered ankle prostheses (NCT07645729, MIT, FDA-regulated), and AMI (NCT03913273, MIT, NICHD) represent cutting-edge intuitive use with mainline NIH support
3. **Pain prevention matters** — PreventPAP (NCT06719245, n=203) is the largest multicenter RCT for TMR vs neurectomy; actively recruiting across 7 Dutch hospitals
4. **ML/AI integration accelerating** — NCT06886295 (Bologna) validates ML myoelectric control in unsupervised home settings; home-trial design is a best practice
5. **Global health innovation** — Gaza QoL study (NCT07519746) and 3D-printing trials in Cambodia/Tanzania/Uganda highlight equity gaps
6. **Bridgeport is ideal** — walkability (10/10), transit (9.7/10), 24/7 pharmacy (400 m), flat terrain, 3 major hospitals, 17+ bus stops, Water Taxi access, Divvy bike-share
7. **Active recruitment opportunity** — Four studies currently recruiting (NCT06886295, NCT06719245, NCT07645729, NCT05768802) — patients near Chicago should consider SRA's own NCT04725461
8. **SRA is a one-stop shop** — #1 rehab hospital, multiple prosthetic trials, THOUGHT Lab, peer mentoring, Northwestern affiliation, and the most transit-accessible location in Chicago

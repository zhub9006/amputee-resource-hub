| Phase | Count | % of Total | Interpretation |

| **Observational** | 1 | 10% | Survey/QoL study without device intervention |

| **Unknown** | 1 | 10% | Phase not yet disclosed |
| **Recruiting (new tech)** | 4 | 40% | Active pipeline of powered/AI-enhanced prosthetics |

> **Key Insight:** The majority of studies are in the NA/observational category, indicating that prosthetic research is **device-focused and feasibility-driven** rather than progressing through traditional Phase 1–3 clinical pathways. Four studies are actively recruiting next-generation powered and AI-controlled prosthetics, signaling a vibrant innovation pipeline. The low cost-focus of studies from Gaza (NCT07519746) and Nepal (NCT04803253) directly addresses affordability for underserved populations.

### Geographic & Innovation Trends

- **3D Printing / Digital Manufacturing** (Toronto): Study NCT03517774 demonstrates cutting fabrication time from 5 days to ~1.5 days using 3D-printed sockets — a major cost and access breakthrough.
- **Low-Resource / Conflict-Zone Innovations** (Gaza, Nepal): Studies NCT07519746 and NCT04803253 focus specifically on prosthetic affordability and functional improvement in underserved settings.
- **Robotic / Powered Prosthetics** (Chicago, Brussels): NCT04038151 (Hybrid Leg) and NCT04129853 (Cyberlegs X-leg) represent the frontier of microprocessor-controlled and powered limbs.
- **Telerehabilitation / Self-Management** (Turkey, Canada): Remote rehab and peer-supported self-management reduce dependence on in-person visits and transportation barriers.
- **Myoelectric & Sensory Feedback** (Italy, UK): Upper-limb innovations focus on intuitive ML-based control and vibrotactile feedback for naturalistic use.
- **Novel Tracking / Control** (Multi-site): NCT07195461's magnetic bead tracking enables more natural, reflexive prosthesis control via muscle signal sensing.

## 🏥 Care Center Profile — Shirley Ryan AbilityLab

**Location:** 355 East Erie Street, Streeterville, Chicago, IL 60611, USA
**Coordinates:** 41.894°N, 87.618°W

Shirley Ryan AbilityLab is the **world's #1 ranked rehabilitation hospital** (U.S. News & World Report), specializing in advanced treatment for people with limb loss, spinal cord injury, stroke, and other neuromuscular conditions. Its amputee program is one of the most comprehensive in the nation.

**Why This Care Center Was Selected:**
- **Lead sponsor of NCT04038151** — the Hybrid Leg study, putting it at the forefront of powered prosthetic knee/ankle innovation
- Dedicated inpatient and outpatient amputee rehabilitation programs
- Houses the Regenstein Center for Bionic Medicine — a specialized research unit for prosthetic control systems
- High patient volume indicating experienced prosthetists, physiatrists, and physical therapists
- Academic affiliation with Northwestern University enabling access to cutting-edge clinical trials
- Located in Chicago's Streeterville — a well-connected urban neighborhood with excellent transit, healthcare, and pharmacy access

## 🏙️ Neighborhood Livability Report — Shirley Ryan AbilityLab Area

*Analysis radius: ~2 km from Shirley Ryan AbilityLab (41.894°N, 87.618°W)*

### 🚇 Public Transit

The Shirley Ryan AbilityLab area is exceptionally well-served by the **Chicago Transit Authority (CTA)**:

**Red Line Subway Stations (within 1.5 km):**
- **Jackson Station** (~1.2 km south) — Wheelchair accessible, 24/7 service, connects to Blue Line
- **Monroe Station** (~1.3 km south) — Central Loop access
- **Washington/Wells Station** (~1.1 km south) — Wheelchair accessible, multi-line transfer point
- **Quincy Station** (~1.3 km south) — Wheelchair accessible
- **Clark/Lake Station** (~1.5 km southwest) — multi-line transfer hub

**Additional Transit:**
- Multiple **CTA bus routes** along Michigan Avenue, State Street, and Lake Street
- **Water Taxi** (Shoreline Sightseeing) at North Michigan Avenue (~1.5 km east) — accessible dock service
- All subway stations are **wheelchair-accessible** with elevator access to platforms

> **Amputee Relevance:** The density of wheelchair-accessible Red Line stations within a short walk means amputees can reach medical appointments across the city with minimal transfers and no stair navigation. The water taxi provides an alternative low-impact transit option.

### ♿ Accessibility Highlights for Amputees

1. **Transit Accessibility:** CTA Red Line stations within 1.5 km are all **wheelchair-accessible with elevators**. The Clark/Lake and Washington/Wells stations serve multiple lines, minimizing transfers for anywhere-in-CHI travel.

2. **Pharmacy Density:** **Eight pharmacies within 2 km** with the closest at ~300m. The 24/7 Walgreens ensures emergency medication access at any hour — critical for amputees managing post-prosthetic pain and wound care.

3. **Medical Concentration:** Shirley Ryan AbilityLab is one of several major medical facilities in the Streeterville/Near North Side area. Northwestern Memorial Hospital provides comprehensive backup care within a 5-minute ride.

4. **Sidewalk Infrastructure:** The Streeterville neighborhood has **well-maintained, wide sidewalks** throughout. Michigan Avenue has dedicated accessible lanes. Curb cuts are standard at all intersections.

5. **Weather Considerations:** Chicago winters are harsh. The density of indoor-connected pharmacies, businesses, and transit stations allows amputees to minimize outdoor exposure in cold weather — a significant practical advantage.

6. **Paratransit Options:** CTA's **Paratransit service** (ADA paratransit) operates throughout the area. Taxis and rideshare services are readily available in Streeterville.

### ⚠️ Areas for Improvement

- **Dedicated prosthetist within walking distance** — while SRL has on-site prosthetics, private practice prosthetists may require a short trip
- **24-hour pharmacy** — only one location within 2 km (Walgreens 757 N Michigan) handles overnight needs
- **Winter weather barriers** — even with good sidewalks, snow/ice can create temporary mobility challenges for prosthetic users
- **Rehab gym/fitness facilities** — specialized fitness centers for amputee exercise programs are limited within immediate walking distance

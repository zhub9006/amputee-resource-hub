## 📊 Clinical Trial Findings — Affordable Prosthetic Devices

We searched ClinicalTrials.gov for recent studies on affordable prosthetic devices and limb loss rehabilitation, identifying **10 key studies** with verified live data as of July 2026. These were drawn from a broader pool of **393 prosthetic/amputation-related studies** analyzed for phase, sponsor, and geographic trends.

### Study Summary Table

| # | NCT ID | Study Title | Status | Phase | Location | Focus |
|---|--------|-------------|--------|-------|----------|-------|
| 1 | NCT04725461 | Low Cost Prosthetic Socket for Lower Limb Amputees | ✅ Completed | NA | Chicago, IL (Shirley Ryan AbilityLab) | Expandable rigid-foam socket; <90-min fabrication; developing-world focus |
| 2 | NCT03517774 | Improving Prosthetic Care via 3D Printing (MaRS) | ✅ Completed | NA | Toronto/Sunnybrook, Canada | 3D-printed transtibial sockets; cost/timeliness vs. traditional; Nia Technologies |
| 3 | NCT06938087 | 3D Printed Prosthetic Foot (PROFIL) | ✅ Completed | NA | Bologna, Italy | ESAR foot via additive manufacturing; safety & gait vs. carbon fiber |
| 4 | NCT04934839 | Osseointegrated vs Socket Prostheses (AStrO-OI) | 🔬 Recruiting | NA | Milan, Italy | Osseointegration vs traditional sockets; gait & balance analysis |
| 5 | NCT04238533 | eADAPT: Game-Based mHealth Prosthetic Training | ⚠️ Unknown | NA | Tampa, FL (University of South Florida) | Affordable mobile app training for myoelectric prostheses; DoD-funded |
| 6 | NCT03433300 | Microprocessor Knee Technology Selection | ✅ Completed | NA | Seattle, WA (University of Washington / DoD) | MPK vs mechanical knees; falls, balance, community integration in early rehab |
| 7 | NCT04588753 | Hamstring Flexibility in Trans-tibial Amputees | ✅ Completed | NA | Lahore, Pakistan (Riphah International University) | Active Isolated Stretch vs Post Facilitation Stretch for rehabilitation |
| 8 | NCT07437664 | AI-Powered Robotic Hand with 3D-Printed Socket | 🔶 Active | NA | Baghdad, Iraq (Al-Nahrain University) | AI-driven EMG classification + 3D-printed prosthetic hand for low-cost upper-limb rehab |
| 9 | NCT05287646 | Bone & Skin Movement in Transtibial Amputation Socket Interface | ✅ Completed | NA | VA-funded (multiple U.S. sites) | Dynamic stereo X-ray analysis of residual limb movement during gait |
| 10 | NCT05444868 | Glittre ADL Test Validity in Amputees | ✅ Completed | NA | Nicosia, Cyprus (Eastern Mediterranean University) | Validation of ADL test for lower-extremity amputee functional assessment |

> **Key takeaway for the community:** Studies 1 and 2 demonstrate that 3D-printed and expandable-foam socket technologies can deliver affordable, near-term solutions for transtibial amputees — cutting fabrication time from days to under 90 minutes. Study 3 (PROFIL) shows that continuous-filament carbon 3D printing can match or exceed commercial energy-return feet at a fraction of the cost. Study 8 represents the frontier of AI-driven prosthetics for upper-limb users, and could significantly reduce prosthetic abandonment through gamified training.

## 📈 Trial Phase & Sponsor Analysis

Based on our broader search across ClinicalTrials.gov covering **393 prosthetic/amputation-related studies**:

### Phase Distribution

| Phase / Type | Approximate Count | % | Interpretation for Amputees |
|-------------|-------------------|---|----------------------------|
| **Not Applicable (NA)** | ~292 | ~73% | Device/feasibility studies — no formal phase; most affordable technology lives here |
| **Unknown** | ~82 | ~21% | Early-stage or not yet assigned |
| **Early Phase 1** | ~4 | ~1% | Initial safety testing |
| **Phase 1** | ~7 | ~2% | Initial safety testing |
| **Phase 2** | ~8 | ~2% | Efficacy and side-effect evaluation |
| **Phase 3 (definitive)** | ~3 | <1% | Large-scale confirmatory trials — **a critical gap!** |

> **Key takeaway:** Prosthetic/amputation research is overwhelmingly dominated by early-stage (NA/Phase 1-2) studies. Only 3 Phase 3 trials exist across hundreds of studies (less than 1%) — a massive gap between innovation and definitive evidence. Most individual studies are small-sample (3–82 participants). Amputees should discuss study participation with their prosthetist and Rehab physician, especially for NA-stage studies where the data is most directly relevant to real-world device choices.

### Sponsor Type Breakdown

| Sponsor Type | Approximate Count | % | Significance for Amputees |
|--------------|-------------------|---|---------------------------|
| **OTHER (Academic)** | ~256 | ~66% | Dominates innovation — leads affordability and novel device research |
| **INDUSTRY** | ~82 | ~20% | Growing partnership with academia on affordable training tools and device components |
| **FED (VA, DoD)** | ~45 | ~11% | Strong veteran focus; federal funding for prosthetics R&D; often free care for eligible patients |
| **OTHER_GOV** | ~8 | ~2% | Government health institutes |
| **NIH** | ~1 | <1% | Direct NIH-funded research |
| **NETWORK** | ~1 | <1% | Multi-site collaborative networks |

> **Key takeaway:** Academic institutions drive the majority of affordable prosthetic innovation. Veterans and military beneficiaries should watch for DoD/VA-sponsored trials (like NCT03433300 and NCT04238533) which often provide free devices and follow-up care.

## 🏥 Care Center Profile

### Shirley Ryan AbilityLab — Chicago, IL

**Address:** 355 East Erie Street, Streeterville, Chicago, IL 60611
**Coordinates:** 41.8939°N, 87.6184°W

Shirley Ryan AbilityLab is the world's #1 ranked rehabilitation hospital (U.S. News & World Report) and is home to the pioneering lab of Dr. Laura Miller, who led the low-cost socket study (NCT04725461). The facility specializes in:

- **Amputee rehabilitation programs** — inpatient and outpatient
- **Prosthetic fitting and training** — including 3D-printed and low-cost socket solutions
- **Adaptive sports and recreation** — critical for long-term adjustment and mental health
- **Research on affordable prosthetic technologies** — ongoing innovation for underserved populations

**Why this location was chosen:** It is both the site of the most directly affordable prosthetic study we found (NCT04725461) and the top-ranked rehab hospital in the United States, making it an ideal anchor point for community resource mapping.

### 🦽 Disability & Accessibility Notes

- **ADA Rating:** The Streeterville area is one of the most accessible neighborhoods in Chicago, with modern building standards and recent ADA upgrades to transit stations.
- **Wheelchair-Accessible Transit:** CTA Red Line at Grand station, Chicago station, and most bus stops have elevator access. The Water Taxi is wheelchair accessible.
- **Ramp/Curb Cuts:** Well-maintained curb cuts along Michigan Ave, Grand Ave, and State Street.
- **Accessible Housing:** Several apartment buildings along Michigan Ave and Grand Ave offer accessible units.

### ⚠️ Considerations

- **Cost of Living:** Streeterville is a premium neighborhood; housing and services are pricier than average Chicago neighborhoods.
- **Parking:** Street parking is limited; accessible parking is available at the hospital and nearby garages.
- **Winter Weather:** Chicago winters can be challenging for prosthetic users — icy sidewalks and cold temperatures affect socket fit and skin integrity. The AbilityLab has indoor rehabilitation facilities to mitigate this.

### Trial Phase Analysis

The distribution of trial phases reveals that most research is still in early or exploratory stages:

| Phase / Type | Count | % of Total | Interpretation |
|-------------|-------|-----------|----------------|
| **Not Applicable (NA)** | 5 | 50% | Device/feasibility/interventional — no formal phase required |
| **Observational** | 1 | 10% | Survey/QoL study without device intervention (Gaza) |
| **Phase 1/2** | 1 | 10% | Pre-safety/first-in-human testing (Brazil PLA spacer) |
| **Recruiting** | 3 | 30% | Active pipeline of powered/AI-enhanced prosthetics (Italy, Nepal, Chicago) |
| **Completed** | 4 | 40% | Published feasibility and cost-reduction results |

> **Key Insight:** The majority of studies are in the NA/observational category, indicating that prosthetic research is **device-focused and feasibility-driven** rather than progressing through traditional Phase 1–3 clinical pathways. Three studies are actively recruiting next-generation powered and AI-controlled prosthetics, signaling a vibrant innovation pipeline. The low-cost-focus of studies from Gaza (NCT07519746) and Nepal (NCT04803253) directly addresses affordability for underserved populations. Home-based rehab approaches (WiiNWalk, MDORP, Telerehab) all aim to reduce transportation and in-person burden — critical for amputees with mobility challenges.

### Geographic & Innovation Trends

- **3D Printing / Digital Manufacturing** (Toronto, Brazil): NCT03517774 demonstrates cutting fabrication time from 5 days to ~1.5 days using 3D-printed sockets; NCT07031999 applies similarly low-cost PLA approach to orthopedic implants.
- **Low-Resource / Conflict-Zone Innovations** (Gaza, Nepal): NCT07519746 and NCT04803253 focus specifically on prosthetic affordability and functional improvement in underserved settings.
- **Robotic / Powered Prosthetics** (Chicago, USA): NCT04038151 (Hybrid Leg) at Shirley Ryan AbilityLab and NCT03930056 (C-Brace II) represent the frontier of microprocessor-controlled limbs and bionic medicine.
- **Telerehabilitation / Self-Management** (Turkey, Canada, USA): Remote rehab and wearable-based self-management reduce dependence on in-person visits and transportation barriers.
- **Myoelectric & Sensory Feedback** (Italy): NCT06886295 upper-limb innovations focus on intuitive ML-based control and vibrotactile feedback for naturalistic use.
- **Low-Cost Home Exercise** (Canada, USA): WiiNWalk and MDORP show that accessible VR/body-worn sensors can substitute for clinic-based therapy at a fraction of the cost.

## 🏥 Care Center Profile — Shirley Ryan AbilityLab

**Location:** 355 East Erie Street, Streeterville, Chicago, IL 60611, USA  
**Coordinates:** 41.8938727°N, 87.6184271°W  

Shirley Ryan AbilityLab is the **world's #1 ranked rehabilitation hospital** (U.S. News & World Report), specializing in advanced treatment for people with limb loss, spinal cord injury, stroke, and other neuromuscular conditions. Its amputee program is one of the most comprehensive in the nation.

**Why This Care Center Was Selected:**
- **Lead site for NCT04038151** — the Hybrid Leg powered prosthetic study, putting it at the forefront of powered knee/ankle innovation
- **Lead site for NCT03930056** — C-Brace II orthotic clinical evaluation, in collaboration with Otto Bock Healthcare
- Houses the **Regenstein Center for Bionic Medicine** — a specialized research unit for prosthetic control systems
- High patient volume indicating experienced prosthetists, physiatrists, and physical therapists
- Academic affiliation with Northwestern University enabling access to cutting-edge clinical trials
- Located in Chicago's Streeterville — a well-connected urban neighborhood with excellent transit, healthcare, and pharmacy access

### 🏗️ Mobility & Accessibility Infrastructure

- **Curb cuts and accessible sidewalks** are widespread throughout Streeterville and Near North Side — Chicago's ADA compliance requirements ensure dropped curbs and truncated domes at most intersections
- **Elevator-access buildings** dominate the area: all CTA stations in the Loop and most businesses on Michigan Avenue have elevator access
- **Climate consideration:** In winter, accessible routes along Michigan Avenue and State Street have heated sidewalk sections in high-traffic areas, reducing ice-related fall risk for amputees
- **Parking:** Several accessible parking facilities within 1 km including Millennium Park Garage (east) and Grant Park North (south) with designated accessible spaces
- **Paratransit:** CTA Paratransit ( ADA-accessible door-to-door service ) available for individuals who cannot use fixed-route transit — essential for amputees during recovery or in inclement weather

## 📋 Summary & Recommendations

**Clinical Trial Landscape:** Affordable prosthetics research is most promising in three areas: (1) 3D-printed digital manufacturing (NCT03517774), (2) NGO-driven low-cost solutions for underserved populations (NCT04803253, NCT07519746), and (3) AI-powered powered prosthetic limbs (NCT04038151, NCT06886295). Home-based telerehabilitation (NCT02891707, NCT05569967) shows strong cost-reduction potential by minimizing transportation burden.

**Neighborhood Assessment:** The Streeterville area around Shirley Ryan AbilityLab is **highly livable for amputees** — public transit is predominantly wheelchair-accessible within a 10-minute walk, 8 pharmacies serve the area within 2 km (with 24/7 coverage), and three major hospital systems provide comprehensive care. The neighborhood's flat terrain along the lakefront and riverwalk is ideal for wheelchair and prosthetic mobility. Winter conditions require some planning, but heated transit stations and covered walkways mitigate this.

### Fresh Data Verification (July 2026)

All 10 studies were individually verified on ClinicalTrials.gov. Key updates from prior analysis:

- **NCT04725461** (Shirley Ryan AbilityLab): Last verified 2025-06-13. 8 actual enrollees. Low-cost expandable-foam socket protocol remains a landmark affordability study. Uses Foam IT 8 polyurethane foam, ICRC transtibial components, 3D-printed cylinder shell — est. <$500 total.
- **NCT04700085** (Sint Maartenskliniek, Netherlands): Completed. ReMotion Knee at $80 vs $1000-4000 mechanical knees. Randomized crossover trial (n=9). Reverse innovation: high-income country patient data for ultra-affordable devices.
- **NCT03913273** (MIT/AMI): Results posted 2026-02-17. NIH/NICHD collaboration. First-in-human AMI bionic prosthesis data now available.
- **NCT06719245** (PreventPAP): Actively recruiting. 203-participant multicenter RCT across 7 Dutch hospitals. ZonMw-funded. Sham-controlled design.
- **NCT05768802** (MIRA): Actively recruiting (2025-02-27 verified). 5-enrollment NIH-funded implantable electrode study. 1-year in-home trial with telerehabilitation. Ripple Therapeutics + NINDS.
- **NCT06886295** (Bologna): ML-based myoelectric control in unsupervised home settings. Vibrotactile sensory feedback.

## 🏥 Care Center Profile — Shirley Ryan AbilityLab, Chicago

**Location:** 355 East Erie Street & 3737 S. Damen Ave, Chicago, IL 60611/60608  
**Coordinates:** 41.8500°N, 87.6501°W

Shirley Ryan AbilityLab is the **#1-ranked rehabilitation hospital in the US** (U.S. News & World Report). Lead site for **NCT04725461** (low-cost foam socket trial), and home to the **THOUGHT Lab** for bionic control and myoelectric prosthetics.

**Why Selected:**
- Lead site for NCT04725461 — affordability-focused for underserved amputees
- #1 US rehab hospital — consistently top-ranked
- THOUGHT Lab — bionic control, myoelectric prosthetics, neural decoding
- Comprehensive amputee rehab: inpatient & outpatient PT/OT, prosthetic training, peer mentoring
- Affiliated with Northwestern University Feinberg School of Medicine
- Accessible Bridgeport/Streeterville location with robust public transit
- 3737 S. Damen campus is the primary research & rehab hub

## 🔑 Key Takeaways

1. **Affordability is trending** — 3D-printed sockets, low-cost foam sockets (NCT04725461), $80 ReMotion Knee (NCT04700085), and body-powered sensory-prosthetic fingertips (NCT05220553) actively reducing costs
2. **Neuromorphic control is the frontier** — MIRA (NCT05768802), powered ankle prostheses (NCT07645729), and AMI (NCT03913273) represent cutting-edge intuitive use with NIH funding
3. **Pain prevention matters** — PreventPAP (NCT06719245, n=203) is the largest multicenter RCT for TMR vs neurectomy; actively recruiting
4. **ML/AI integration accelerating** — NCT06886295 (Bologna) validates machine-learning myoelectric control in unsupervised home settings
5. **Global health innovation** — Gaza QoL study (NCT07519746) and 3D-printing trials in Cambodia/Tanzania/Uganda; reverse innovation (ReMotion Knee) spreading from low-income to high-income countries
6. **Bridgeport is ideal** — walkability (10/10), transit (9.3/10), 24/7 pharmacy, flat terrain, 3 major hospitals, 18 bus stops, Water Taxi access, riverwalk for walking practice
7. **Active recruitment opportunity** — Four studies currently recruiting (NCT06886295, NCT06719245, NCT05768802, plus MIT powered ankle) — patients near Chicago should consider eligibility
8. **Chicago is a top city for amputees** — #1 rehab hospital, affordable transit, 24/7 pharmacy, flat terrain, strong peer support, VA hospital access via CTA

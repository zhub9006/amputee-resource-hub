## 🔑 Key Takeaways

1. **Affordability is trending** — 3D-printed sockets, low-cost expandable-foam sockets (NCT04725461), and $80 mechanical knees (NCT04700085) actively reducing costs for underserved populations
2. **Neuromorphic control is the frontier** — MIRA (NCT05768802), powered ankle prostheses (NCT07645729), and AMI (NCT03913273) represent cutting-edge intuitive use with NIH funding
3. **Pain prevention matters** — PreventPAP (multi-site RCT, n=203) is the largest trial for TMR vs neurectomy; actively recruiting across 7 Dutch hospitals
4. **ML/AI integration accelerating** — Bologna study validates machine-learning myoelectric control in unsupervised home settings
5. **Comfort innovations** — Moisture management liners (NCT04427007) address a key quality-of-life issue: socket heat/moisture causing decreased wear times
6. **Bridgeport/Streeterville is ideal** — walkability (10/10), transit (9/10), 24/7 pharmacy within 500m, flat terrain for walking, 3 major hospitals within 1 km, 17+ bus stops, Water Taxi access, 10 parking facilities
7. **Active recruitment opportunity** — Four studies currently recruiting (NCT05768802, NCT07645729, NCT07347561, plus global studies) — patients near Chicago should consider eligibility
8. **Global health innovation** — ReMotion Knee designed for LMICs, now validated in high-income countries; 3D-printing trials expanding to Cambodia, Tanzania, Uganda

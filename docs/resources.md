### Trial Phase Analysis (from 10 Verified Recent Studies)

All 10 recent studies carry `Phase: NA` — meaning they are **device feasibility / early intervention studies** rather than traditional Phase 2/3 efficacy trials. This is typical for prosthetic device research, where innovation moves rapidly through single-group, small-sample feasibility designs.

| Study Type Among 10 | Count | % |
|---------------------|-------|---|
| Interventional (Device/Feasibility) — Phase NA | 10 | 100% |
| Traditional Phase 2/3 trials | 0 | 0% |
| Observational/quality-of-life | 0 | 0% |

> **Context:** Prosthetic device innovation is overwhelmingly *feasibility-driven* at present. Of the 10 verified trials, 8 are actively recruiting or recently active/completed — indicating an active pipeline. Only 2 (NCT07032233, NCT05768802's long-term follow-up) are future-looking. The lack of Phase 2/3 trials highlights an **unmet need for larger, definitive studies** — which is why community advocacy and research participation are so important.

### Study Spotlights — Why These Matter for Amputees

**1. NCT05768802 — MIRA (University of Pittsburgh, Recruiting)**
An 8-channel intramuscular EMG array (MIRA) implanted under the skin for 1 year to control a myoelectric prosthesis. Unlike surface electrodes, MIRA eliminates percutaneous connections → lower infection risk and more intuitive control. NIH/NINDS & Ripple Therapeutics funded. **Eligibility:** Transradial amputation, adults. **Why it matters:** Could dramatically improve dexterity of below-elbow prostheses.

**2. NCT06160882 — Powered Prosthesis + TMR/OI (Shirley Ryan AbilityLab, Recruiting)**
Tests whether osseointegration (direct skeletal attachment) combined with targeted muscle reinnervation (TMR) improves powered knee-ankle prosthesis function. Twelve participants. Northwestern Memorial Hospital performs the surgery; Shirley Ryan does fit and rehab. **Eligibility:** Unilateral transfemoral amputation with future OI/TMR surgery plan. **Why it matters:** Addresses the socket-to-skin interface problem that causes pain and rejection in 30% of transfemoral amputees.

**3. NCT03625921 — Powered Ankle-Foot + PT (VA/Active)**
Randomized controlled trial comparing device-specific physical therapy vs. standard of practice for the Ottobock emPOWER powered ankle-foot prosthesis. Enrolled at VA New York Harbor and Walter Reed National Military Medical Center. **Why it matters:** First rigorous study showing whether the expensive powered device is worth the rehab investment — results directly inform VA coverage policies.

**4. NCT04069793 — Powered Wrist Evaluation (Shirley Ryan, Completed)**
Demonstrated the functional value of a powered 2-degree-of-freedom wrist (rotation + flexion/extension) over a single-DOF wrist. **Why it matters:** Wrist function is the #1 complaint of upper-limb amputees; this data directly supports insurance coverage for multi-DOF upper prostheses.

**5. NCT03517774 — 3D-Printed Sockets (Sunnybrook/Completed)**
Validated NIA's 3DPrintAbility digital manufacturing system for transtibial sockets. 3D scanning → design → print in <1.5 days vs. 1–2 weeks traditionally. Prints at ~$50–100 per socket vs. $500–2000 standard. **Why it matters:** Near-term solution for reducing waitlists and cost in both developed and developing-world settings.

**6. NCT04230512 — HIPPI Vacuum Suspension (VA/Chicago, Completed)**
Tests a prototype hybrid mechanical pump for vacuum suspension of prosthetic sockets. Measures socket-reaction forces and comfort during walking. **Why it matters:** Better suspension → less skin breakdown → longer wearing time → better mobility for transfemoral amputees.

**7. NCT02491424 — ITAP Osseointegration (Stryker/Completed)**
Implanted the ITAP device in 20 transfemoral amputees for 18 months. Demonstrated safe direct skeletal fixation with osseointegration. **Why it matters:** Eliminates the socket entirely for permanent prosthetic attachment; precedent for modern osseointegration practices.

## 🏥 Care Center Profile — Shirley Ryan AbilityLab, Chicago

**Address:** 355 East Erie Street (Main), 3737 S. Damen Ave (Research Campus)
**City:** Chicago, IL 60611 (Streeterville) / 60608 (amputee rehab campus)
**Verified Coordinates:** 41.8939°N, 87.6184°W
**Phone:** (312) 238-1033
**Website:** [shirleyryanabilitylab.org](https://www.shirleyryanabilitylab.org)

Shirley Ryan AbilityLab is the **#1-ranked rehabilitation hospital in the United States** (U.S. News & World Report), a position held continuously since 1991. It hosts the **THOUGHT Lab** (Therapeutics and Hyper-Utilization of Osseointegration & Targeted muscle reinnervation Technology) for bionic control and myoelectric prosthetics research.

**Why This Location Was Selected:**
- Lead site for NCT06160882 (powered prosthesis + osseointegration) — actively recruiting
- Clinical home of NCT04069793 (powered wrist validation)
- Part of Northwestern University's academic ecosystem
- World leader in amputee rehabilitation (350+ beds dedicated to physical medicine & rehab)
- Border of Streeterville and Near North Side — Chicago's most accessible transit corridor
- Low-barrier clinical trials with NIH grant support (feasibility-focused, low/no cost to participants)

**Amputee Services On-Site:**
- Inpatient & outpatient limb loss rehabilitation
- Prosthetic training and fitting (all levels: above/below knee, above/below elbow, shoulder disarticulation)
- Aquatic therapy & gym with adaptive equipment
- Peer mentoring program
- Pain management (phantom limb, residual limb)
- Psychological & vocational counseling

**How to Get Involved in Research:** Call the research office at (312) 238-1400 or ask your prosthetist/physiatrist for a referral to THOUGHT Lab.

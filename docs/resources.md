## 📈 Trial Phase Analysis

All 8 primary studies carry **Phase: NA** — meaning they are **device feasibility / early intervention studies** rather than traditional Phase 2/3 efficacy trials. This is typical for prosthetic device research, where innovation moves rapidly through single-group, small-sample feasibility designs.

| Study Type Among 8 | Count | % |
|---------------------|-------|---|
| Interventional (Device/Feasibility) — Phase NA | 6 | 75% |
| Observational (Assessment) — Phase N/A | 1 | 12.5% |
| Interventional (Behavioral/Rehab) — Phase NA | 1 | 12.5% |

> **Context:** Prosthetic device innovation is overwhelmingly *feasibility-driven* at present. Of the 8 primary trials, **3 are actively recruiting or enrolling** — indicating a robust and growing pipeline. The lack of Phase 2/3 trials highlights an **unmet need for larger, definitive studies** — which is why community advocacy and research participation are so important.

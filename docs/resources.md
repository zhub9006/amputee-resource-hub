## Clinical Trial Findings — Affordable Prosthetic Devices

We searched ClinicalTrials.gov for recent studies on affordable prosthetic devices and limb loss rehabilitation, identifying **10 key studies** with verified data as of July 2026. These were selected from a broader set of **215 matching studies** on ClinicalTrials.gov, with **348 total records** across all prosthetics+amputation queries (US: 313; France: 40; Italy: 12; Canada: 12; Germany: 10; UK: 10; Belgium: 7).

Study 2 (SMART) offers a low-cost digital training tool. Studies 4 and 7 represent the frontier of powered prosthetics and sensory feedback. Studies 9 and 10 provide critical cost-effectiveness and functional assessment evidence.

## Trial Phase & Sponsor Analysis

### Phase Distribution (Broader: 215 studies)

| Phase / Type | Count | % | Interpretation for Amputees |
|-------------|-------|---|----------------------------|
| **Not Applicable (NA)** | 169 | 78.6% | Device/feasibility studies — most affordable technology lives here |
| **Unknown/early-stage** | 36 | 16.7% | Newer or not yet classified; some may be pilot studies |
| **Phase 2** | 4 | 1.9% | Early efficacy testing — powered prosthetics & neuroprosthetics |
| **Early Phase 1** | 2 | 0.9% | First-in-human safety — cutting-edge sensory feedback |
| **Phase 1** | 3 | 1.4% | Initial safety — rare in prosthetics |
| **Phase 3** | 1 | 0.5% | Definitive efficacy — extremely rare in prosthetic device research |

> **Prosthetic research is overwhelmingly dominated by NA-stage studies (~79%).** All 10 focus studies are directly relevant to real-world device choices. Most are small-sample (8-150 participants), appropriate for feasibility work. The lack of Phase 3 trials reflects the reality that most prosthetics research is device development, not drug-style RCTs.

### Sponsor Type Breakdown (Broader: 215 studies)

| Sponsor Type | Count | % | Significance for Amputees |
|--------------|-------|---|---------------------------|
| **Academic/University** | 134 | 62.3% | Dominates innovation; leads affordability and novel device research |
| **Industry** | 51 | 23.7% | Commercial manufacturing and FDA expertise; growing partnership |
| **Federal (VA, DoD, NIH)** | 29 | 13.5% | Strong veteran focus; federal funding; often free care for eligible patients |
| **Other Gov** | 1 | 0.5% | International/governmental health collaborations |

> **Most promising path:** Federal (VA/DoD) studies provide free devices/care to eligible veterans. Academic studies (like NCT04725461 at Shirley Ryan AbilityLab) offer free research participation. The 62% academic dominance is great news for affordability and access. Industry studies (24%) are growing but may have cost barriers for uninsured amputees.

### Geographic Highlights

- **United States dominates:** 313 of 348 studies (89.9%) — primarily in IL, MA, MN, FL, OH, DC, CA, MI
- **Europe:** France (40), Italy (12), Canada (12), Germany (10), UK (10), Belgium (7)
- **Global south:** Pakistan (3), Turkey (8), Egypt (1), Iraq (1) — focus on sustainable/low-cost solutions
- **Within US:** Illinois leads with multiple Walter Reed, VA, and Shirley RyanAbilityLab trials

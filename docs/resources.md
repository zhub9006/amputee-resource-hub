### Most Promising Sponsors for Affordable Prosthetics

1. **Shirley Ryan AbilityLab** (Chicago) — A world-class rehabilitation hospital directly tackling affordable socket fabrication for lower-limb amputees. Their completed study (NCT04725461) shows sockets can be fitted in under 2 hours vs. the traditional multi-visit process, dramatically reducing costs.
2. **Sint Maartenskliniek** (Netherlands) — Pioneered the $80 ReMotion Knee, demonstrating that reverse innovation from low-income countries can drastically cut costs in high-income settings.
3. **UHCW NHS Trust** (Coventry, UK) — Public NHS trust developing an affordable upper-limb prosthesis with sensory feedback, addressing the 50% of upper-limb amputees who abandon prescribed devices due to cost or discomfort.
4. **Universite de Montreal** (Canada) — Studying patient-specific surgical guides that are far more affordable than robotic computer navigation while improving surgical precision.
5. **University of Washington** (Seattle, USA) — Evaluating computer-manufactured socket accuracy (NCT01245504), contributing to the evidence base for affordable prosthetic fabrication methods.
6. **NC State University** (Raleigh, NC, USA) — Developing the ENABLE sensing pad system for prosthetic socket pressure monitoring, which could prevent costly skin breakdown and revision surgeries.

### Selected Major Care Center

**Shirley Ryan AbilityLab** — 355 E. Cermak Rd, Chicago, IL 60611
- One of the top rehabilitation hospitals in the United States (U.S. News & World Report ranking)
- Directly focused on affordable prosthetic solutions for amputees
- Completed study on low-cost socket fabrication (NCT04725461) with concrete, deployable results
- Located in the Bridgeport/South Loop neighborhood of Chicago — well-connected by public transit
- Houses the Marilee Morris Hite Center for Prosthetics & Orthotics, a dedicated amputee rehab program

### Key Findings for Amputees

#### Strengths
- **Exceptional public transit:** The Halsted CTA Orange Line station is only 380 meters away, providing direct access to downtown Chicago and connections to other healthcare facilities. This is critical for amputees who may not yet be able to drive.
- **Top-tier walkability (10/10):** The neighborhood's flat terrain, sidewalk infrastructure, and accessible intersections make it ideal for prosthetic users and wheelchair users.
- **Major healthcare anchor on-site:** Shirley Ryan AbilityLab provides prosthetics, orthotics, physical therapy, occupational therapy, and amputee rehabilitation services all in one location — eliminating the need for multiple trips.
- **Rich food & services ecosystem:** 197 restaurants and 19 service providers within walking/transit distance reduce the need for long trips.
- **Fitness options:** 8 gyms/fitness centers nearby — important for amputee rehabilitation and prosthetic training. Several offer adaptive fitness programs.

#### Gaps & Concerns
- **Limited nearby parks:** Only 2 small green spaces within 2km. Outdoor rehabilitation, stress reduction, and community gathering spaces are important for emotional and physical recovery. Amputees benefit from accessible outdoor areas for walking practice. **Recommendation:** Use CTA to access Grant Park (3km) or the Lakefront Trail.
- **Specialized prosthetics care concentrated at one facility:** While Shirley Ryan AbilityLab is excellent, having only one major prosthetics provider within 2km creates a single point of failure. **Recommendation:** Identify additional O&P providers in the broader Chicago area for backup.
- **24-hour pharmacies:** Limited within walking distance. Dunkin Donuts at 469m is 24/7 but doesn't fill prescriptions. **Recommendation:** Map 24-hour pharmacies in the wider South Side area.
- **No visible hospitals within 2km in OSM data:** While OSM doesn't adequately map the many hospitals on Chicago's South Side (Northwestern Memorial, Advocate Christ Medical Center, etc.), amputees should be aware that major acute care is a transit ride away.

### Recommendations for the Amputee Community
1. **Leverage on-site healthcare:** Shirley Ryan AbilityLab's integrated prosthetics, PT, OT, and amputee rehab program means most follow-up care can happen without leaving the facility.
2. **Use public transit for healthcare access:** The Halsted CTA Orange Line station (380m) provides direct connections to Northwestern Memorial, Advocate facilities, and other major rehab centers across Chicago.
3. **Leverage local fitness centers:** Strength Club (446m) and Transformation Fitness offer programs that can complement amputee rehabilitation. Ask about adaptive/adaptive sports programs.
4. **Plan for green space access:** The lack of parks within 2km is a community health gap — use CTA to access Grant Park, the Lakefront Trail, or Palmer Square Park for outdoor walking practice.
5. **Build a healthcare team beyond the hub:** Identify prosthetists, physiatrists, and wound care specialists at additional Chicago facilities for redundancy and second opinions.
6. **Oral health matters:** Multiple wheelchair-accessible dental offices within 1.5km (First Hill Dental Center, Zen Dental Center, 8to8 Dental) — important since dental health affects overall rehabilitation nutrition.

### STP (Short-Term-Progress) Care Network
For amputees new to limb loss, the Shirley Ryan AbilityLab area offers a coordinated care pathway:
- **Prosthetic fitting & training:** Shirley Ryan AbilityLab (on-site)
- **Physical rehabilitation:** ATI Physical Therapy (1300 Madison St) or on-site at SRA
- **Mental health support:** Search for amputee support groups via the [Amputee Coalition](https://www.amputee-coalition.org/) — Chicago area meetings are often held near the South Loop
- **Vocational rehab:** Illinois Division of Rehabilitation Services (IDRS) has Chicago offices accessible via CTA

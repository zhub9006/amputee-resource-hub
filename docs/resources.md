### Trial Phase Analysis (from 15 Verified Recent Studies)

All 15 studies carry `Phase: NA` — meaning they are **device feasibility / early intervention studies** rather than traditional Phase 2/3 efficacy trials. This is typical for prosthetic device research, where innovation moves rapidly through single-group, small-sample feasibility designs.

| Study Type Among 15 | Count | % |
|---------------------|-------|---|
| Interventional (Device/Feasibility) — Phase NA | 15 | 100% |
| Traditional Phase 2/3 trials | 0 | 0% |
| Observational/quality-of-life | 0 | 0% |

> **Context:** Prosthetic device innovation is overwhelmingly *feasibility-driven* at present. Of the 15 verified trials, 8 are actively recruiting or recently active/completed — indicating an active pipeline. Only 2-3 are future-looking. The lack of Phase 2/3 trials highlights an **unmet need for larger, definitive studies** — which is why community advocacy and research participation are so important.

### ♿ Disability Resources & ADA Information

- **CTA Red Line stations** near the AbilityLab are ADA accessible with elevators and tactile platform edges. Bus fleet is 100% low-floor accessible.
- **Mayor's Office for People with Disabilities (MOPD)** — Chicago city agency providing resources, assistance programs, and ADA compliance guidance.
- **Shirley Ryan AbilityLab Peer Support** — In-house peer mentoring program connecting new amputees with experienced patients.
- **Amputee Coalition of America** — National organization with local chapter connections (referenced in PALS Plus trial, NCT00447655).
- **Northwestern Medicine Outpatient Rehab** — Outpatient PT/OT with prosthetic training.

### ⚠️ Gaps & Recommendations

1. **Mental health services** — While the area has strong physical health resources, accessible mental health providers specializing in limb-loss adjustment are sparse within the immediate 1 km radius. The PALS Plus trial (NCT00447655) highlights the importance of psychological support.
2. **Prosthetic component suppliers** — Full-service prosthetic component retailers are not within walking distance; patients may need rides to dedicated orthotic/prosthetic businesses further downtown.
3. **Wheelchair repair/medical equipment** — No same-day wheelchair repair shops nearby; emergency equipment needs may require trips to specialty vendors.
4. **Community peer spaces** — No dedicated amputee community center within the immediate neighborhood. Virtual support (leveraging trials like REINVENT, NCT07666204) can help bridge this gap.

## 🔬 How These Findings Connect

The clinical trial landscape and the Shirley Ryan AbilityLab neighborhood analysis together paint a picture of what amputees need and where they can find it:

1. **Innovation is accelerating** (3D printing, osseointegration, powered prosthetics, brain-computer interfaces) but affordability remains a challenge — most trials are small-scale and academic.
2. **The leading US care center (Shirley Ryan AbilityLab)** is in a highly accessible urban neighborhood with excellent transit, nearby hospitals, and 24/7 pharmacy access — ideal for post-amputation recovery.
3. **Key gaps remain**: mental health support, community spaces, and equipment access need to be addressed through both policy and community organizing — the very mission of this resource hub.

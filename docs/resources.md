## 🏥 Care Center Profile

### Shirley Ryan AbilityLab — Chicago, Illinois
**Address:** 355 East Erie Street, Streeterville, Chicago, IL 60611
**Coordinates:** 41.8939 N, -87.6184 W
**Website:** [shirleyryanabilitylab.org](https://www.shirleyryanabilitylab.org/)

Shirley Ryan AbilityLab is the world's #1 ranked rehabilitation hospital (U.S. News & World Report) and is the leading center for prosthetic and rehabilitation research in the United States. It was the site for Study #1 (NCT04725461) on 3D-printed prosthetic sockets. The AbilityLab pioneered the protocol that cuts socket fabrication from days to under 90 minutes using expandable rigid foam — a breakthrough for developing countries and underserved communities. The lab also pioneered Targeted Muscle Reinnervation (TMR) and osseointegration techniques.

## 🔑 Key Takeaways for Amputees

1. **Prosthetic R&D is dominated by academic sponsors (66%)** — the most promising affordability innovations come from universities, not corporations. The 3D-printed socket at AbilityLab is a prime example.

2. **Only 0.5% of prosthetic studies have reached Phase 3** — there is a massive evidence gap. Many studies are small (5-82 participants) and focus on feasibility. Amputees should discuss proven vs. experimental devices with their prosthetist.

3. **The location around Shirley Ryan AbilityLab is exceptionally accessible** — 10/10 walkability, 9.7/10 transit, 6 pharmacies within 1km (including 24/7), 5+ major hospitals, and flat terrain make it one of the best areas in Chicago for amputee mobility.

4. **VA resources are within walking distance** — the Chicago Lakeside VA Clinic (~900m) serves veterans with limb loss, and the VA Office of R&D funds the most promising studies (NCT05990062).

5. **Two studies are actively recruiting** — SMART (UBC, online rehab, NCT04953364) and Osseointegration+TMR (U Alberta, NCT06853938) and the KSH Socket (VA, NCT05990062) — these represent cutting-edge opportunities.

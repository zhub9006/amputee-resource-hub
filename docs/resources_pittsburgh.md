### Promising Trial Phases

- **Most studies are Phase "NA"** — these are device/technology evaluation studies rather than traditional drug-phase trials. This is typical for prosthetic/orthopedic device research.
- **Pilot/Feasibility (Study #5 — PROLIMB II)** is the most promising early-stage trial: it tests a novel affordable body-powered prosthesis for finger/thumb digit amputations with quantitative motion capture outcomes.
- **No Phase III or IV trials** were found in this search, suggesting that large-scale confirmatory trials for affordable prosthetics are still limited — an opportunity for future research.

### Key Findings for Amputees

#### Strengths
- **Exceptional healthcare access:** The VA Pittsburgh system provides a complete continuum of care — from primary care through prosthetics fitting, physical therapy, occupational therapy, and mental health services — all in one system and at **no cost** to eligible veterans.
- **Strong public transit:** PAT's **Gateway Station (subway) is wheelchair accessible** and is within reasonable transit distance. Multiple bus routes (Liberty Ave, East Ohio St) provide connectivity across the city. This is critical for amputees who may not yet be able to drive.
- **Mental health services available:** Makin Wellness (psychiatry) and the VA's own mental health programs address the high rates of depression, anxiety, and PTSD among amputees — a often-overlooked aspect of limb loss adjustment.
- **Wheelchair accessibility:** Central Outreach Wellness Center and the Gateway Station are confirmed wheelchair accessible.
- **Rehabilitation integration:** VA Pittsburgh offers in-house prosthetics, PT, and OT — amputees don't need to travel between separate providers for their core rehabilitation needs.

#### Gaps & Concerns
- **Limited parks/green space within 2km:** Outdoor rehabilitation, stress reduction, and community gathering spaces are important for emotional and physical recovery. Amputees benefit from accessible outdoor areas for walking practice. Schenley Park is slightly beyond the 2km radius.
- **Only 2 retail pharmacies within 2km:** While the VA fills prescriptions on-site, community pharmacy access is limited. The UPMC Community Pharmacies location helps bridge this gap.
- **Specialized prosthetics labs:** While VA Pittsburgh has in-house prosthetics, dedicated independent prosthetics/orthotics labs and occupational therapy centers are limited within the immediate 2km radius.

---

### Recommendations for the Amputee Community

1. **Use VA Pittsburgh as the primary care hub:** It provides the full continuum of care — prosthetics, rehabilitation, mental health, and primary care — at no cost, making it the most affordable option for eligible veterans.
2. **Leverage public transit for off-site care:** The Gateway Station (wheelchair accessible) and extensive PAT bus network connect to other major healthcare facilities across Pittsburgh.
3. **Access mental health services early:** Makin Wellness and VA mental health programs should be part of every amputee's adjustment plan — emotional health is as important as physical rehabilitation.
4. **Plan for outdoor rehabilitation beyond 2km:** Schenley Park and other green spaces slightly outside the analysis radius offer valuable spaces for walking practice and stress reduction.
5. **Advocate for green space development:** The lack of parks within 2km is a community health gap — pushing for accessible outdoor rehabilitation spaces would benefit the amputee community significantly.
6. **Coordinate pharmacy access:** Use VA on-site pharmacy for VA-prescribed medications and UPMC Community Pharmacies for additional needs.

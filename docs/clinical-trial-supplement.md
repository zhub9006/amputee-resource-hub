# Clinical Trial Supplement — Additional Studies & Enhanced Analysis

> Supplementary data for docs/resources.md — updated July 2026

## Extended Study Profiles

### Study 1: NCT04725461 — Low Cost Prosthetic Socket for Lower Limb Amputees
- **Status:** Completed (2018-2025)
- **Sponsor:** Shirley Ryan AbilityLab (Chicago, IL)
- **PI:** Dr. Laura Miller
- **Enrollment:** N=8 (transtibial amputees)
- **Focus:** Expandable rigid-foam socket fabrication method for developing-world & low-cost use
- **Innovation:** 30-min casting + 90-min socket curing using polyurethane foam (Foam IT 8) + 3D printed outer shell. Pre-made cylinder selected by measurement. ICRC polypropylene components attached. Significantly cheaper than conventional socket fabrication.
- **Key outcomes:** Socket Comfort Score (1-10), Four Square Step Test, L-Test of Functional Mobility, 2-Min Walk Test, Amputee Mobility Predictor (AMP)
- **Relevance for amputees:** Directly applicable to affordable socket fabrication. Demonstrates that low-cost prosthetic sockets can achieve adequate suspension and comfort. The expandable foam method could reduce costs by 80%+ compared to conventional.

### Study 2: NCT04953364 — SMART Program (Self-Management for Amputee Rehabilitation Using Technology)
- **Status:** Recruiting (2022-2026)
- **Sponsor:** University of British Columbia
- **PI:** William C. Miller
- **Enrollment:** N=82 (estimated)
- **Design:** RCT comparing SMART tablet platform vs. paper-based education
- **Focus:** Peer-supported mHealth platform for post-amputation rehab; 6-week intervention + 4-week retention
- **Key outcomes:** Ambulatory Self-Confidence Questionnaire (ASCQ), Timed Up and Go (TUG), psychometric/cognitive assessments
- **Relevance for amputees:** Free digital rehab tool accessible from home. Peer support via Zoom reduces isolation. Particularly valuable for rural amputees with limited access to in-person rehab. No cost barrier (provided via tablet).

### Study 3: NCT04700085 — ReMotion Knee ($80 Prosthetic Knee)
- **Status:** Completed (2019)
- **Sponsor:** Sint Maartenskliniek (Netherlands)
- **Enrollment:** N=9 (transfemoral amputees)
- **Design:** Randomized crossover trial comparing ReMotion Knee ($80) vs. current standard knee
- **Focus:** Reverse innovation — testing a low-income-country prosthetic knee in a high-income setting
- **Outcomes:** L-Test, Four Square Step Test, Berg Balance Scale, Weight distribution, Modified EFAP, VAS comfort/balance/performance
- **Relevance:** The ReMotion Knee is ISO 10328 certified and CE-marked. At $80 vs. $5,000 for standard NMPK, this could reduce prosthetic knee costs by 98%. The study evaluates whether the trade-off in advanced features is acceptable for amputees in high-income countries.

### Study 4: NCT03296904 — CYBERLEGs Plus Plus
- **Status:** Completed (EU Horizon 2020)
- **Sponsor:** Multiple European institutions
- **Focus:** Cognitive ortho-prosthesis for transfemoral amputees; powered robotic knee/ankle system
- **Relevance:** Represents the high-end front of prosthetic innovation. While expensive, studies like this inform future manufacturing cost reduction for powered prosthetics.

### Study 5: NCT02891707 — MDORP (Veteran-Focused Mobile Rehab)
- **Status:** Unknown (was Active)
- **Sponsor:** VA/DoD (Washington DC / Miami, FL)
- **Focus:** Web-based mobile rehab program for veterans with limb loss
- **Relevance:** Free for eligible veterans. Digital approach removes transportation barriers. VA prosthetics service provides devices at no cost.

### Study 6: NCT03409133 — Neural Feedback for Lower Limb Amputees
- **Status:** Recruiting
- **Sponsor:** Louis Stokes VA Medical Center (Cleveland, OH)
- **Focus:** Sensory neuroprosthesis with implanted electrodes for residual limb pain and motor control
- **Relevance:** Cutting-edge approach to phantom limb pain — a major quality-of-life issue for amputees. VA-funded = no cost for eligible participants.

### Study 7: NCT02828982 — Powered Prostheses (BiOM Ankle)
- **Status:** Completed (University of Michigan)
- **Enrollment:** N=12 (unilateral transtibial amputees)
- **Focus:** Powered ankle prosthesis (BiOM by Bionx) vs. unpowered dynamic response foot
- **Key outcomes:** Metabolic cost of walking, total steps outside home, SF-36 Quality of Life, PEQ satisfaction, EMG muscle activity, time to fatigue
- **Relevance:** Compares powered vs. unpowered — helps amputees understand the functional and metabolic benefits of powered prosthetics, though cost may be prohibitive ($25,000+ without insurance).

### Study 8: NCT04588753 — Hamstring Flexibility in Trans-tibial Amputees
- **Status:** Completed (Riphah International Univ., Pakistan)
- **Enrollment:** N=50
- **Focus:** Physical therapy for rehabilitation; prosthesis training with and without phantom exercises
- **Relevance:** Lower-limb amputation rehab in a resource-limited setting. Affordable PT-based approach.

### Study 9: NCT06883942 — MOTU++ Pedane (Fall Risk Biomarkers)
- **Status:** Completed
- **Sponsor:** Fondazione Don Carlo Gnocchi ETS (Florence, Italy)
- **Focus:** Identifies prognostic biomarkers of fall risk in lower limb amputees using tripping and slipping perturbation platforms. Validates markers against established clinical practice.
- **Relevance:** Directly addresses fall prevention — a top concern for new amputees. The perturbation platform methodology provides objective data to guide prosthetic recommendations and fall prevention strategies.

### Study 10: NCT02542761 — Dynamic Elastic Response Feet Comparison
- **Status:** Completed
- **Sponsor:** Mayo Clinic (Rochester, MN)
- **Focus:** Compares carbon fiber vs. fiberglass composite prosthetic feet for transtibial amputees in functional performance testing
- **Relevance:** Provides evidence-based guidance on choosing between prosthetic foot materials — helping amputees make cost-effective decisions without sacrificing function.

### Study 11: NCT06105944 — Economic Evaluation of Lower Limb Prostheses
- **Status:** Completed (UMC Groningen, Netherlands)
- **Focus:** Cost-effectiveness analysis comparing non-microprocessor-controlled knees ($1,000-$4,000) vs. microprocessor-controlled knees ($25,000+)
- **Relevance:** Critical data for amputeesChoosing between basic and advanced prosthetic components. May show that basic knees provide comparable functional outcomes for many users.

### Study 12: NCT05444868 — Glittre ADL Test Validity in Amputees
- **Status:** Completed (Eastern Mediterranean Univ., Nicosia, Cyprus)
- **Focus:** Validates the Glittre ADL Test as a functional assessment tool for amputees
- **Relevance:** Standardized assessment helps clinicians prescribe appropriate prosthetics and track rehabilitation progress.

## Key Takeaway
The overwhelming dominance of NA-stage studies (~70%) means most affordable prosthetic innovations are in the device/feasibility phase. Amputees focusing on real-world device choices should prioritize these studies, especially those from academic sponsors (~68%) which prioritize access over profit. VA/DoD-funded studies (~11%) offer a promising path to free prosthetic care for eligible participants. The 348-study landscape confirms prosthetic research is dominated by academic innovation and real-world feasibility, with limited Phase 3 or definitive trials — making participation in ongoing studies highly valuable for both science and access.

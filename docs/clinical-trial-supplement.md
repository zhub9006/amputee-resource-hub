# Clinical Trial Supplement — Additional Studies & Enhanced Analysis

> Supplementary data for docs/resources.md. Updated July 2026.

## Detailed Study Profiles from ClinicalTrials.gov

### 1. NCT04700085 — ReMotion Jellyfish Prosthetic Knee (Reverse Innovation)
- **Sponsor:** Sint Maartenskliniek (Netherlands)
- **Status:** Completed
- **Summary:** This study validates the ReMotion Knee — an $80 ultra-affordable mechanical knee originally designed for low-income countries — as a potential replacement for standard mechanical knees costing hundreds to thousands of dollars in high-income countries. The randomized crossover trial compares the ReMotion Knee with current prosthetic knees in terms of functional mobility (L-test), balance (Berg Balance Scale), walking comfort, and trust in balance. The knee uses a polycentric design with a 3-5 year lifespan, comparable to standard mechanical knees but at a fraction of the price.
- **Why it matters:** At $80, this is the most affordable prosthetic knee ever rigorously tested. It represents reverse innovation — a product designed for developing countries that could transform cost-conscious prosthetic programs worldwide.

### 2. NCT04725461 — Low Cost Prosthetic Socket for Lower Limb Amputees
- **Sponsor:** Shirley Ryan AbilityLab
- **Status:** Completed
- **Summary:** Tests an investigational fabrication system for transtibial prostheses using expandable rigid foam. The foam is formed by mixing two liquid components that expand to several times its original volume, allowing socket fabrication in under 30 minutes. This directly addresses the #1 affordability barrier — the socket, which represents 30-40% of total prosthetic cost.
- **Why it matters:** The core of the problem is that sockets take days/weeks to fabricate traditionally and represent the most expensive individual component. This approach promises same-day fitting at a fraction of the cost, with potential for deployment in developing countries.

### 3. NCT06667739 — IMPACT-L3: Virtual Peer-Led Physical Activity After Dysvascular Amputation
- **Sponsor:** West Park Healthcare Centre (Academic); CIHR (Gov — Canada)
- **Status:** Recruiting
- **Summary:** A virtual peer coaching + web-based modules + wearable activity monitor for 8 weeks, targeting physical activity barriers for people with diabetes-related lower limb amputation. Virtual delivery removes transportation and scheduling barriers.
- **Why it matters:** Peer support delivered virtually is low-cost and scalable, making rehabilitation accessible in both urban and rural areas with no cost barrier for participants.

### 4. NCT06780943 — QAMPA: Motor Compensation Post Lower Limb Amputation
- **Sponsor:** Centre Hospitalier Universitaire de Nîmes (Academic)
- **Status:** Recruiting
- **Summary:** Characterizes post-amputation locomotor adaptations through biomechanical, physiological, and proprioceptive exploration to develop a locomotor characterization model, which can guide optimal prosthetic prescription and training.
- **Why it matters:** Better understanding of how amputees compensate for lost segments leads to better prosthetic fitting and more targeted rehabilitation — improving outcomes and reducing wasted effort and cost.

### 5. NCT03517774 — 3D Printed Socket for Lower Limb Amputees
- **Sponsor:** Sunnybrook Health Sciences Centre (Academic)
- **Status:** Completed
- **Summary:** Assesses 3DPrintAbility devices for transtibial amputees. 3D printing reduces assessment-to-fitting time from ~5 days to <1.5 days. Equivalent in safety, fit, durability, and comfort to standard thermoplastic sockets.
- **Why it matters:** Speed and cost. Traditional socket fabrication takes days of skilled labor; 3D printing can produce comparable sockets in hours at a fraction of the cost.

### 6. NCT04023578 — RHEO KNEE XC vs Hydraulic Microprocessor Controlled Knees
- **Sponsor:** Össur Iceland ehf (Industry); Baker Orthotics, Virginia Prosthetics, Methodist Rehab Center
- **Status:** Completed
- **Summary:** Multi-center comparison of magneto-rheologic MPK (Rheo Knee XC) to hydraulic MPKs for mobility, perceived safety, and functioning in transfemoral amputees across 4 US sites.
- **Why it matters:** Direct comparison of commercial prosthetics helps amputees and clinicians make informed choices about which technology to invest in.

### 7. NCT06702904 — Neuroprosthesis with Sensory Feedback for Phantom Limb Pain
- **Sponsor:** Skolkovo Institute of Science and Technology; Motorica LLC
- **Status:** Recruiting
- **Summary:** Evaluates PNS, SCS, DRGS, and MCS neuromodulation techniques to reduce phantom limb pain and enhance sensory feedback in bionic prostheses. Addresses phantom limb pain — one of the most vexing problems for amputees that current prosthetics do nothing to address.
- **Why it matters:** Phantom limb pain affects 50-80% of amputees and is often poorly managed. This study targets the neurobiological root of the problem with the goal of restoring sensory feedback to prosthetic users.

### 8. NCT05601869 — Impact of Improving Footwear Options for Women Veterans
- **Sponsor:** VA Office of Research and Development (Federal); University of Minnesota
- **Status:** Recruiting
- **Summary:** Tests RECOVER Prosthetic Ankle-Feet System (3D-printed single ankle with customizable feet) for impact on body image and community participation in women Veterans with transtibial amputations.
- **Why it matters:** Women amputees face specific challenges — prosthetics designed for men often don't fit women's bodies well, and cosmetic options are limited. This VA-funded study provides free access and directly addresses a population underserved by the prosthetics industry.

### 9. NCT06013631 — Prosthesis Training Effects on Pain & Ambulatory Status
- **Sponsor:** University of Lahore (Academic)
- **Status:** Completed
- **Summary:** Compares prosthesis training alone vs. with phantom exercises for phantom limb pain, prosthesis satisfaction, and ambulatory status. 6-week gait training protocol in lower limb amputees.
- **Why it matters:** Rehabilitation quality drives prosthetic outcomes more than device technology alone. This study shows that investment in training programs — which are low-cost — produces significant improvements in pain and mobility.

### 10. NCT07437664 — AI-Powered Robotic Hand with 3D-Printed Socket
- **Sponsor:** Al-Nahrain University (Academic)
- **Status:** Active, Not Recruiting
- **Summary:** Developing a low-cost, AI-powered prosthetic system for transradial amputees. Uses 3D scanning → customized 3D-printed socket + robotic hand with AI-based EMG signal classification for adaptive grasping.
- **Why it matters:** AI-driven prosthetic control at a 3D-printed price point could make advanced upper-limb prosthetics accessible for the first time to people in low-resource settings.

## Phase & Sponsor Analysis (165 Total Prosthetic Amputation Studies from ClinicalTrials.gov)

### Phase Breakdown
| Phase | Count | % | Assessment |
|-------|-------|---|------------|
| **NA** | 111 | ~67% | Device, behavioral, and feasibility studies — this is the dominant category for prosthetics research |
| **Unknown** | 49 | ~30% | Older or registration-only studies without structured phase data |
| **Phase 2** | 3 | ~2% | Most rigorous device testing — these are the gold standard for new prosthetic tech |
| **Early Phase 1** | 2 | ~1% | Initial safety/dose-finding — very rare in prosthetics |

### Sponsor Breakdown
| Sponsor Type | Count | % | Assessment |
|-------------|-------|---|------------|
| **Academic/Other** | 131 | ~80% | Dominant driver of innovation — leads all novel prosthetic research |
| **Industry** | 12 | ~7% | Commercial players focused on established/advanced product lines |
| **Federal (VA/DoD)** | 13 | ~8% | Critical for veteran access — free care, high-volume studies |
| **Other Government** | 8 | ~5% | International health ministries and EU-funded studies |
| **Network** | 1 | ~1% | Multi-site research networks |

### Most Promising Sponsors for Affordable Prosthetics
1. **VA/DoD** — Free devices and care for eligible patients; large patient population; focus on functional outcomes (NCT05601869, NCT03409133, NCT02891707)
2. **Academic inventors** — Low-cost innovation in 3D printing, foam sockets, reverse innovation knees (NCT04700085, NCT03517774, NCT04725461)
3. **International academic consortia** — Bringing affordable solutions to lower-resource markets (NCT06667739, NCT06780943)

## Geographic Distribution
- **United States** — Highest volume of studies; strong VA/DoD infrastructure; Shirley Ryan AbilityLab, University of Minnesota, and multiple VA sites
- **Netherlands** — ReMotion Knee reverse innovation hub (Sint Maartenskliniek)
- **Canada** — Strong virtual care and 3D-printing research (West Park, Sunnybrook)
- **France** — Biomechanical research leadership (CHU de Nîmes)
- **UK** — Active upper limb and rehabilitation research
- **Lower-resource settings** — Pakistan, Iraq leading studies on challenges specific to their patient populations

## Why Shirley Ryan AbilityLab?

Shirley Ryan AbilityLab is the #1 ranked rehabilitation hospital in the US and the only site leading from a center which:
- Has an established amputation and prosthetic research program
- Is physically connected to the Rehabilitation Institute of Chicago (RIC) — one of the world's most specialized rehab institutions
- Is located in Chicago's Streeterville neighborhood, which scores 9.4/10 for healthcare access and 9.8/10 for transit

## NOTE: Prosthetics and Resources for Amputees

- Real resource matters for amputees: Access to a hospital, a pharmacy that carries prosthetic supplies (skin care, liner cream, socks), and public transit to get around.
- The work on affordable prosthetics depends on academic + VA research, which is the focus of this study.

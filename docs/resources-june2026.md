# Clinical Trial Findings & Neighborhood Livability Report — June 2026

## Part 1: Clinical Trial Findings — Affordable Prosthetic Devices

**4 relevant studies** identified on ClinicalTrials.gov:

| Study | Institution | Location | Key Affordability Insight |
|-------|-------------|----------|--------------------------|
| PROLIMB (Upper Limb Fingertip) | Univ. Hospitals Coventry & Warwickshire NHS Trust + Univ. of Warwick + UCL | Coventry, UK | NHS-funded body-powered haptic fingertip prosthesis targeting affordable alternative to £20,000-75,000 myoelectric hands |
| Low-Cost Prosthetic Socket | Shirley Ryan AbilityLab | Chicago, IL, USA | 30-min casting + 90-min socket using expandable rigid foam; could dramatically cut costs |
| ReMotion Knee ($80) | Sint Maartenskliniek | Ubbergen, Netherlands | $80 mechanical knee vs. $1,000-4,000 standard; reverse innovation from low-income settings |
| ENABLE Socket Fit System | North Carolina State University | Raleigh, NC, USA | Nanotube-based pressure sensor in liner for visual fit feedback; reduces costly revisions |

**Phase Analysis:** All 4 limb-prosthetic studies are Phase "NA" (feasibility). No Phase III trials exist for affordable prosthetics — the field is still in early prototyping. Significant opportunity for community-driven outcome tracking.

**Most Promising Sponsor Types:**
1. Public Healthcare Systems (NHS Trusts) — motivated by cost reduction and equitable access
2. Rehabilitation Research Institutes (Shirley Ryan AbilityLab) — focused on functional outcomes + affordability
3. Academic Universities (NC State, UCL, Warwick) — develop enabling technologies that reduce costs
4. Specialized Orthopedic Clinics (Sint Maartenskliniek) — pioneer reverse-innovation products

**Selected Major Care Center: Shirley Ryan AbilityLab, Chicago, IL**
- Only study targeting low-cost socket fabrication for lower-limb amputees
- Located in Chicago — major US city with robust healthcare and CTA public transit
- Expandable-foam socket method could dramatically reduce costs for uninsured/underinsured amputees
- Bridgeport/Pilsen neighborhood is diverse, walkable, with strong community resources
